### 1.6 Social Media Policy
#### Objective(s)
- The policy aims to inform associates about appropriate behavior on social media and protect the company from legal or security issues.
- It sets expectations for behavior to prevent legal problems or public embarrassment and outlines how associates should conduct themselves online.
- Social media posts should be channeled through authorized individuals to protect the organization's reputation.

#### Scope and Applicability
- This policy applies to all associates, who are expected to comply at all times to protect the privacy, confidentiality, and interests of the company, its services, associates, partners, customers, and competitors.

#### Guidelines/Rules for Usage of Social Media
- The company reserves the right to restrict or prevent access to certain social media websites if personal use is deemed excessive.
- Monitoring of social media use is conducted to the extent necessary to uphold these guidelines.

### 4.1 Social Media Policy

All associates of the organization are expected to abide by the following rules regarding their social media presence and activities:

1. **Brand Ambassadorship**: As an associate of Trianz, you act as an ambassador for the brand. Your social media activity should not harm the company's brand.
   
2. **Work Commitments**: Ensure that social media usage does not interfere with business or work commitments.

3. **Terms of Use**: Read and comply with the terms of use of any social media platform you utilize.

4. **Observation Before Participation**: Before contributing to any social media site, observe the activity to understand the style, content, and any unwritten rules.

5. **Honesty and Accountability**: Be honest and open in your posts, but be mindful of their impact on perceptions of the company. Promptly admit and correct any mistakes.

6. **Personal Responsibility**: You are personally responsible for the content you publish on social media, which will be public for many years.

7. **Caution in Publishing**: If you feel uneasy about something you are about to publish, do not post it. Discuss any doubts with your line manager and, if necessary, the Head of HR.

8. **Respect Privacy**: Consider others' privacy and avoid discussing inflammatory topics such as politics, religion, caste, community, and gender.

9. **Legal Responsibility**: You may be legally responsible for your posts; therefore, respect brands, trademarks, and copyrights.

10. **Confidential Information**: Never disclose commercially sensitive, anti-competitive, private, or confidential information. If unsure, consult your line manager or Head of HR.

11. **Third-Party Content**: Do not upload, post, or forward content belonging to a third party without their consent.

12. **Conflict Resolution**: Do not escalate heated discussions. Be conciliatory and respectful, and correct misrepresentations with facts. Avoid contributing when angry or upset.

13. **Personal Data Protection**: Refrain from publishing personal or sensitive information about clients, associates, or vendors that could be widely accessed.

14. **Reporting Critical Content**: Report any critical content about the company on social media to the Head of HR.

15. **Authorized Communication**: Associates are prohibited from speaking on behalf of the organization on social media without prior permission. Only authorized personnel may communicate on behalf of the company.

16. **Discretion with Media**: Exercise special discretion when posting photographs or videos involving associates or offices to protect their data privacy.

### Disciplinary Action

Breach of this policy may result in serious disciplinary action and discussions with HR.

### 2.1 Purpose

### 2.2 Recruitment Procedure

- The recruitment team manages the hiring and selection process.
- The recruitment team receives hiring requests from business/functional heads based on the strategic plan and ongoing business needs.

### 2.2 Recruitment Process

- **Initial Screening**: Recruiters perform initial profile screening of resumes, which are then sent to the hiring manager for shortlisting.
- **Interview Process**: Shortlisted candidates may undergo one or more of the following interview types:
  - Personal round of interview (face-to-face)
  - Telephonic round of interview followed by a personal round
  - Video/web-conference based interview
- **Assessment Process**: Depending on the requirement and experience level, candidates may be required to complete:
  - Written test
  - Aptitude test
  - Group discussion/Panel interview
  - Personal interview
- **Freshers (Campus Recruits)**: Must undergo a written test, aptitude test, group discussion (optional), and personal interview.
- **HR Interview**: Shortlisted candidates will attend an HR interview, and employment consideration is based on the interview results.

### 2.3 Notice Pay Reimbursement

- **Notice Period Buy-Out**: The notice period of a candidate can be purchased based on business needs and urgency. The amount is calculated based on the notice days purchased.
- **Offer Letter Requirement**: The notice period buy-out must be explicitly mentioned in the offer letter, and the associate must submit required documents to claim reimbursement.
- **Recovery Clause**: If an associate who availed the notice period buy-out leaves the company within 12 months of joining, the entire amount will be recovered from their final settlement.

### 2.4 Relocation Policy (for New/Transferred Associate)

- **Purpose**: To assist associates in mitigating costs associated with domestic relocation due to business requirements.
- **Scope**: Applicable to new joiners and associates on transfer.

#### Relocation Expenses

- **Calculation**: Based on the distance from the current work location or residence to the new location.
- **Coverage**: Includes transit expenses, travel tickets, and initial accommodation for the associate and family.

##### a. Transit Expenses

- **Eligible Expenses**:
  - Cost of moving personal goods by railway or road
  - Cost of packing materials
  - Incidental travel costs
  - Transit insurance for goods
- **Limits for Transit Expenses**:
  - Around New Delhi, Gurgaon, Jaipur, Calcutta: INR 35,000 (All levels)
  - Mumbai, Pune, Ahmedabad, Indore, Nagpur: INR 30,000 (All levels)
  - Chennai, Bangalore, Cochin, Hyderabad, Trivandrum: INR 20,000 (All levels)

##### b. Travel Tickets

- Associates are eligible for travel tickets for their family while relocating, claimable only once within three months from the date of joining.
- Family includes spouse, children, and dependent parents.

#### Eligibility Criteria for Travel Modes

- **Air Travel**: 
  - Economy Class for grade 5 and above.
- **Rail Travel**:
  - Air-conditioned 1st Class for grade 9 and above.
  - AC 2-tier / AC Chair-car / AC 2-tier sleeper / AC-3 tier sleeper for all grades.
- **Bus Travel**: 
  - Air-conditioned coach/Bus-Luxury/Volvo for all grades.
- **Car Travel**: 
  - Train fare/AC bus fare equivalent as per eligibility for all grades.

- **Exceptions for Air Travel**: If not eligible for air travel, associates may avail it with prior approval under specific conditions:
  - Emergency/critical service to customers.
  - Escorting prestigious clients.
  - Travel by rail/road exceeding 18 hours.
  - Rail/road fare equal to or greater than airfare.
  - Operational exigencies necessitating air travel.

- **Public Transport Requirement**: Associates in salary grade 8 and below are expected to use convenient overnight public transport when available.

#### a. Boarding and Lodging

- **Initial Accommodation**: Eligible for accommodation for a maximum of 10 days for the associate and family.
- **Conveyance**: Reimbursement for conveyance from the place of stay to the workplace, subject to a maximum of INR 150 per day.
- **Food Expenses**: 
  - Boarding for initial 10 days not exceeding INR 450 per day for the associate.
  - If relocating with family, the allowance is INR 500 per day, subject to actuals.

#### Entitlement Table of Lodging and Boarding

- **Class of City**: Specific entitlements based on city/area and grade will be defined in the complete policy document.

### 3.9 Work from Home Policy
#### Scope
- The Work from Home (WFH) Policy applies to all authorized Trianz associates and client-approved contract employees using Trianz IT assets and facilities remotely from home, applicable only in India.

#### Definition of Work from Home
- Working from Home is defined as the ability for associates to work from a remote location within India, using Trianz assets and equipment for a prolonged duration.
- Requisite manager approval is mandatory to work from home. Decisions regarding remote work will be made on a case-by-case basis at the discretion of the manager or Trianz management, ensuring compliance with all statutory guidelines and policies.
- Approval to work remotely is at Trianz's discretion and is not a contractual or statutory right.

#### Request to Work Remotely
- All requests must align with the definition of the WFH Policy and comply with statutory guidelines. Documentation related to remote work must be maintained.
- Associates must secure agreement from their line manager before the remote work date. Retrospective requests will not be accepted, and unauthorized absences may lead to disciplinary actions.
- For projects requiring client approvals for remote work, the reporting manager must obtain client approval before granting the work from home request.
- Line managers must ensure a clear business requirement exists for remote work, such as the associate's ability to focus on specific tasks without daily office distractions.

### 10.1 Working Requests for Working from Home

Line managers must ensure that deliverables and performance are tracked while associates work remotely. When an associate initiates a request to work from home, managers should consider the following criteria:

- **Nature of Job**: If the associate’s job requires regular face-to-face contact with other associates or the public, it may be unsuitable for remote work.
  
- **Skills and Abilities**: The associate’s performance should be evaluated to determine suitability for unsupervised work.

- **IT Dependencies**: Associates who depend on organizational IT assets that they do not possess are excluded from this policy.

- **Impact on Team**: Managers must assess the potential impact on colleagues and other teams, ensuring sufficient resources are available to cover the associate’s absence.

- **Home Location Suitability**: The associate’s home must provide a secure working environment, verified by the line manager using a documented checklist before approving the request.

- **Medical Appointments**: Requests to work from home coinciding with medical appointments are allowed if there is a clear business benefit.

- **Flexi-time Recording**: Associates on flexi-time must record hours worked at home in the attendance tracking system.

- **Request Reinitiation**: All work from home requests must be reinitiated by the associate every three months and re-approved by managers, unless changes are made by Trianz management based on criticality. Managers can revoke work from home privileges with two days' notice.

### 10.2 Expectations of Associates Who Work from Home

- Associates must engage in agreed Trianz work and be contactable during regular business hours.

### 10.3 IT Equipment

- Trianz will provide official laptops. Connectivity to the Trianz network or email requires IS configuration, which must be authorized by the project/delivery manager and approved by InfoSec.

### 10.4 Work Location

- All associates are expected to work within India.
- Associates must inform their managers via email if they are not working from the designated location.

### 10.5 Telephone

- Associates must use their own telephones for occasional calls while working from home.

### 10.6 Security Requirements

- Associates must complete all mandatory trainings (e.g., POSH, INFOSEC) before working from home.
- A WFH Checklist must be filled out by the associate and approved by the project/delivery manager, maintained for audit purposes.
- If the associate is an ODC user, an approval email from the client must be kept for audit purposes.
- Associates must be aware of the increased risk of security breaches while working from home. All documentation must be stored securely, and laptops or PCs must be password protected and turned off when not in use.
- IT equipment provided for remote work is for the exclusive use of the associate; sharing with family or friends is prohibited.
- Associates must comply with Trianz policies regarding IT equipment and applications as per the Acceptable Usage Policy & Data Privacy Policy.
- For remote access to Trianz networks/servers, associates must obtain VPN access approval from their manager.

### 10.7 Dress Code

- Associates must dress in professional attire as per the Dress Code Policy during video conferences or client meetings via Skype or other visual media.

### 10.8 Background Noise

### 11.6 Monitoring and Review While on Work from Home
- Line managers are responsible for monitoring associates' performance while working from home.
- Compliance with this policy and its effectiveness will be monitored through audits conducted at least once every six months.
- Compliance with security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy controls is mandatory.
- WFH audit findings, security incidents, and corrective and preventive action plans will be reviewed by the Information Security Assurance Council (ISAC).
- The WFH Policy will be reviewed biannually, with amendments made based on management review findings. If a legislative or security incident occurs, a review will happen within seven working days, and policy changes will occur within 14 days.
- Action closure for security incidents will occur no later than 30 days from the date of the Security Incident Reporting (SIR).
- For any security breaches, the responsible line manager must notify ISAC within 24 hours.
- The InfoSec team will assess the breach's magnitude and impact and provide a SIR report to ISAC.
- ISAC will review the SIR and define the Security Incident Response Plan (SIRP), including client communication.

  - Alleged discrimination based on race, color, sex, age, religion, national origin, marital status, or disability.
  - Improper or unfair administration of associate benefits or conditions of employment (e.g., fringe benefits, promotions, retirement, holidays, performance reviews, salary, or seniority).

#### Procedure for Raising a Grievance
1. An aggrieved associate can raise a grievance via email to their unit head or HR.
2. The Business Head will review the grievance, discuss it with the Project Manager/HR, and communicate the decision to the associate within one day. The decision will be recorded in the grievance form. If the associate is satisfied, the matter is resolved, and the grievance email is sent to HR for filing.
3. If the associate is not satisfied with the Step 1 response, they can escalate the grievance to HR. HR will consult with the Head-HR and communicate the decision within two days. If the associate is satisfied, the grievance is resolved, and the decision is recorded and conveyed.
4. If the associate remains unsatisfied, the grievance will be referred to a committee for a final decision. The committee, coordinated by the Head-HR, will include the Head-HR, Business Head, and an HR representative. The committee will meet to resolve the grievance, and the decision will be communicated by the Head-HR within one day after the meeting. The resolved grievance will be recorded in the associate's personal file.
5. Associates are encouraged to consult with HR, supervisors, or management informally regarding complaints.
6. Information concerning grievances must remain confidential. Only individuals who need to know or provide necessary background information may discuss the grievance.
7. The Company may refuse to proceed with any grievance deemed improper under this policy.

**4. Performance Management**

**4.1 Introduction**
- The Performance Management Process begins with setting goals, aligning organizational objectives with associates’ agreed measures, skills, competency requirements, development plans, and results delivery.
- It is an ongoing process of monitoring, communicating, prioritizing, and measuring objectives, emphasizing improvement, learning, and development to achieve business goals and create a high-performance workforce.

**4.2 Objectives**
The objectives of the Performance Management Process include:
- Providing a structure for communication between associates and the organization to align objectives and aid in realizing career goals.
- Reiterating the organization's commitment to retaining capable associates and recognizing quality performance and meritocracy.
- Offering continuous feedback and encouragement to associates to enhance performance and increase two-way communication between supervisors and associates.
- Clarifying mission, goals, responsibilities, priorities, and expectations to help realize short-term and long-term goals.
- Aligning reward and recognition programs with individual performance.

**Scope:** This policy applies to all permanent full-time associates of Trianz.

**4.3 Performance Management Process**
- The Performance Management Cycle follows a January to December calendar.
- The steps in the Performance Management Process are:
  - Goal setting
  - Confirmation Appraisal
  - Project End Appraisals
  - Annual performance review

**Definitions:**
- **Appraisee:** Associate whose performance is being assessed/reviewed.
- **Appraiser:** Individual responsible for conducting the performance appraisal/review for the appraisee.
- **Reviewer:** Business Unit Head or Functional Head.
- **Competency framework:** Defined competencies for each role in the organization across four levels: Individual Contributor, Leads, Managers, and Senior Leaders.
- **Rating:** Categories include S (Satisfactory), E (Exceeds), M (Meets), B (Below), and I (Improvement Needed).

**4.4 Goal Setting Process**
- Goals are motives and values that drive behavior, describing what individuals and the organization aim to accomplish.
- Goals provide direction and purpose, set for all associates at the beginning of the fiscal year, and for new entrants within a week of joining.
- Reporting managers and team members collaboratively set 4-5 SMART goals with measurement criteria for each goal.
- Inputs for setting goals include:
  - Organizational objectives
  - Roles and responsibilities based on organization and project definitions
  - Goals should help individuals realize core competencies.
- Upon completion, goals are signed off by both the team member and the reporting manager and are reviewed during annual performance reviews.

**Confirmation:**
- All associates are deemed confirmed on their date of joining.

**Project End Appraisals:**
- Goals for project team members are set by the designated project manager at the project's start and reviewed quarterly or at the logical end of the project or when team members are reassigned.

### 4.5 Annual Performance Reviews
- The performance appraisal summarizes and evaluates an individual's overall performance for the year.
- The appraisee's performance is reviewed against the goals set and the competencies defined for their role.
- An overall rating for the individual is determined and discussed during the performance review discussion with the appraisee.
- A developmental plan will be discussed during the performance review.
- The rating serves as one of the inputs for decisions regarding promotions, succession planning, strategic planning, and compensation reviews.

### 4.6 Performance Improvement Plan
- Associates whose performance requires improvement may be nominated for a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).
- The PIP is implemented at the discretion of the reporting manager, BU head, or functional head to assist the associate in improving performance.
- The PIP aims to facilitate constructive discussions between the associate and their supervisor and clarify the performance that needs improvement.

**Process:**
- The appraiser, in consultation with the BU head and HR representative, will:
  - Develop a plan, including timelines for measurement and the purpose of the activities or goals to help the associate achieve the desired performance level.
  - Identify and specify the support and resources needed to assist the appraisee.
  - Specify timelines for performance reviews.
  - State the expected level and consistency of performance from the appraisee and outline possible consequences if performance standards are not met.

- During the PIP period, the appraisee will undergo three reviews (every 30 days) conducted by the appraiser in consultation with the reviewer and HR representative.
- If the associate fails to meet the required improvement plan by the end of the PIP period, appropriate decisions will be made, which may include further extension of the PIP or separation.

**Gross Pay Components:**
- **Basic Salary:**

### 5.3 Ex-Gratia/Bonus
- Ex-Gratia is a voluntary payment initiated by the employer as a mark of goodwill.
- The Ex-Gratia amount is defined by company standards and is a fixed, taxable component paid monthly.
- If an associate is covered under the Payment of Bonus Act, this component will be classified as 'Bonus'; otherwise, it will be classified as 'Ex-Gratia'.

### 5.4 Special Allowance
- The Special Allowance is calculated as follows:
  - Special Allowance = CTC – (Basic + HRA + Ex-Gratia/Bonus + Gratuity + PF (employer’s contribution)).

- As a security policy, associates are not allowed to copy personal data onto a floppy disk or burn a CD without prior approval from the HR head or BU-Head.

#### 6.5 Notice Period
- All associates who resign must serve the full notice period as per the terms and conditions of employment.
- Associates cannot waive the notice period against accumulated leave or pay towards the notice period.
- Waiving the notice period will only be considered if the associate has been relieved from the project by the Project Manager before serving the mandated notice period. Only HR has the discretion to waive the notice period in deemed fit cases.
- HR approval must be obtained for any notice period waiver.
- Notice period recovery will attract prevailing taxes and other statutory requirements as applicable.
- Associates are not recommended to go on leave while serving the notice. If they do take leave, the notice period will be extended by the number of days of leave taken.

#### 6.6 Separation in Case of Absconding Associates
- In the event of unauthorized absence or absconding for more than one day, the manager must inform the HR Business Partner about the associate's absence on the second day.
- On the second day, the HR Business Partner will attempt to contact the associate via the associate’s contact number and/or send an email notification to the associate’s personal email.

### 6.6 Absence and Job Abandonment

- Associates must inform HR via their personal mail ID regarding their absence or the reason for it.
- The HR Business Partner will wait for the associate’s response for one additional day.
- If the associate does not respond by the fourth day, the HR Business Partner will initiate Job Abandonment formalities and inform all stakeholders. Following this, the associate's record will be terminated in the HRMS.
- If the associate expresses the intention to not continue, HR will proceed with the separation process.

### 6.7 Separation in Case of Associate Under Service Agreement

- For associates under a Service Agreement, the HR Representative will initiate the Settlement process within two days of receiving notice of the associate’s resignation.

### 7. Business Continuity Plan

- During the Business Continuity Plan, certain policies may be relaxed based on the situation and decisions made by the Trianz Business Continuity Group.
