# Trianz India Travel Policy

## 1. Overview
Trianz promotes a business culture focused on cost consciousness and sensible spending. This policy serves as a guide for reimbursing business expenses while encouraging associates to demonstrate sound business behavior. The goal is to establish a consistent framework for travel and expense management.

## 2. Purpose
The purpose of this policy document is to:
- Update the travel booking process for tickets, hotel stays, and car rentals.
- Clarify reimbursable and non-reimbursable expenses for associates.
- Ensure fairness and consistency in reimbursement for similar expenses across Trianz.
- Promote responsible spending of client and company funds.
- Maintain proper documentation for internal and client reimbursable expenditures, adhering to standards required by tax authorities.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all India Trianz associates.

### 3.1 General
- All official travel services must be booked through the Trianz travel desk.
- Travel requests must include proper approvals, project codes, and billability details.
- Reservation confirmations will be sent within 48 hours of the request.
- Trianz will not cover additional expenses from travel extensions due to personal reasons.
- Personal travel should not be combined with official travel.

## PART-I Travel Policy - Domestic Travel
- Business travel must be raised using the designated tool and approved in advance according to the approval matrix.
- All official travel services must be booked via the company travel desk. The company will not settle invoices from unauthorized travel agents.
- Associates should select the most economical travel options and utilize advance purchase or fare reduction schemes.
- If opting for a higher fare, justification and exception approval are required.
- If tickets are not arranged by authorized travel agents, prior approval is necessary, and the reason must be stated.

  - For all grades, eligible travel modes are: 
    - ROAD (Air-conditioned coach / Bus – Luxury/Volvo).

- If not eligible for air travel, associates may avail air travel with prior approval from the divisional/business head under the following situations:
  - Emergency or critical service to customers.
  - Escorting prestigious clients.
  - Travel by rail/road taking 18 hours or more.
  - Rail/road fare is more than or equal to airfare.
  - Operational exigencies necessitate air travel.
  - Convenient overnight public transport is available; associates in Salary Group 8 and below are expected to use it (e.g., train for Bangalore – Chennai).

### 4.2 No Show Policy

- Associates must report to the airport/train station well in advance of the departure time.
- All additional expenses incurred due to delayed reporting and no-shows must be borne by the associate.
- Exceptions apply only in cases of medical emergencies where the associate could not communicate in time for ticket cancellation.

### 4.3 Hotels

- The Trianz travel department will book hotels for all company travel. 
- All reservation requests must be made using the online travel request tool.
- Requests made within 48 hours may be booked by the associate, who can choose cost-effective options and will be reimbursed after the trip.
- If associates make their own reservations, they will be limited to lodging rates as per the entitlement table below.

- Accommodations will be made in the Company Guest House or approved hotels for best value, unless:
  - No moderate class hotel is available in the city.
  - The location is not cost-effective (e.g., saving on hotel but spending more on transportation).
  
- The policy does not apply when attending seminars or conventions with pre-booked rooms or when clients insist on making travel arrangements.

- For new reservations or changes while on a trip, associates should first contact the hotel front desk. If additional resources are needed, they should reach out to the travel desk.

- If unable to use a reservation, it is the traveler's responsibility to notify the travel desk for cancellation. Associates should check the hotel's cancellation policy for restrictions.

- "No-show" charges are not reimbursable. Associates from Grade 1 to 4 will, wherever possible, be placed in double occupancy for cost optimization.

### 4.4 Travelling by Own Vehicle
- Associates traveling to another city on business in their own vehicle are entitled to reimbursement of:
  - INR 8 per kilometer, or
  - The cost of the ticket as per their entitlement, whichever is lesser.
- The organization does not recommend traveling by personal vehicle; such travel is at the individual's own risk, and the organization is not liable for any incidents.

### 4.5 Expense Submission
- All expenses and claims must be submitted via the expense reimbursement application within 7 days of trip completion.
- Claims will be approved by the concerned Trianz manager and settled with the finance team.
- Submissions beyond 7 days require special approval from the Head of the Department.
- Claims submitted beyond 30 days may be rejected by the finance team unless valid justification is provided (e.g., medical emergency, business trips).
- Any travel advance not settled within 30 days from the completion date will be recovered in the subsequent month.

### PART-II Travel Policy - Short-Term International Travel
- All air and hotel reservations must be made through the Trianz travel desk.
- Travel requests must be submitted online with proper approvals, project code, and billability details.
- Associates should select the most economical options and utilize advance purchase or fare reduction schemes.
- If a higher fare is chosen, justification and exceptional approval are required.
- Trianz is not responsible for personal accommodations, including flights, hotels, meals, and car rentals.

#### Approval Matrix
- **Type of Travel**: Business
  - **Approver**: Channel Head + Business Finance
  - **Timeline**: Request to be raised at least 2 weeks in advance with necessary approvals.
  
- **Type of Travel**: GTS (Cost of travel to be billed and reimbursed with the client)
  - **Approver**: Delivery Manager/Delivery Head, GTS Head, Business Finance

- **Type of Travel**: Corporate Function
  - **Approver**: Respective SLT + HR Head & Business Finance

- This policy applies to all associates traveling overseas from India to other Trianz offices or client sites internationally.

### Benefits for Associates
- Visa processing for the country of travel.
- Air tickets.
- Medical insurance coverage for the duration of stay overseas.
- Reimbursement of local conveyance (within India/overseas) on actuals.
- Accommodation arrangements in company guest houses or approved hotels within prescribed limits.
- If accommodation is booked by the client, it must be used, and costs will be reimbursed with supporting bills.
- Per diem is provided from the day of arrival in the country until the last day of stay.
- Associates may be placed in double occupancy for cost optimization.

### 5.1 Transit / En-route Expenses
The following expenses are covered:
- Transportation charges between terminals or airports on actuals.
- Telephone charges for official purposes with supporting bills.
- Transit accommodation costs if the stay exceeds 6 hours (within the airport stay is suggested).
- Food expenses on actuals with supporting bills.

### 5.2 Car Rentals
- For the USA, Trianz has rates with car rental companies (Enterprise, National, and Hertz) and can arrange bookings through the travel desk.
- Associates may also book independently using Trianz discount codes:
  - National and Enterprise: XZ23981
  - Hertz: 2069732
- Efforts should be made to book low-cost options.

### 5.2 Car Rental and Transportation

- For Enterprise and National car rentals, Loss Damage Waiver insurance is included in the Trianz Corporate program. If using Hertz or other car rental services, ensure to include Loss Damage Waiver insurance at the time of hiring.
  
- The car must be a compact or mid-size unless more than 2 associates are sharing a car. In that case, a van or mid-size SUV may be rented, with a preference for low-cost options. Rental receipts must be submitted for reimbursement along with necessary approvals.

- If car rentals are not possible, associates should use public transport or the most economical means of transport. Reimbursement will be provided at actual costs upon submission of relevant receipts.

- Reimbursements are only applicable for official travel. In regions outside of the home country, associates are advised to use local transport (train/bus), and such tickets can be claimed for reimbursement at actual costs. Taxis or cabs may be hired if local transport facilities are unavailable.

- Associates combining personal travel with official travel will not be eligible for per diem, accommodation, or any other reimbursements during their leave. Personal travel or weekend travel expenses incurred while using a rental car for personal use are not claimable.

### 5.3 Telephone

- Country-specific calling cards will be provided for associates in grade 8 and above.

- Associates in grade 7 and below are encouraged to purchase local SIM cards in the country of travel, with the cost of all official calls reimbursed at actuals.

### 5.4 Policies – Reimbursable and Non-Reimbursable Expenses

**Reimbursable Expenses:**
- ATM charges for drawing cash from a card.
- Telephone calls made for official purposes to clients and the office in India.
- Dining charges for taking clients or their representatives for lunch/dinner, subject to approval from VP/Director, excluding alcoholic beverages, cigarettes, tobacco, and similar items.
- Local SIM card charges for business requirements, reimbursable up to $25 per month at actuals.
- Parking and toll charges, reimbursable at actuals.

**Non-Reimbursable Expenses:**
- Customs duty on items brought for personal use.
- Usage of sports facilities, laundry, spa, and gym in hotels.
- Charges for TV viewing of specific channels.
- Any other personal expenses.
- International data roaming costs during business travel; associates must disconnect data roaming before international travel.
- Any fines resulting from noncompliance with public rules.

### 5.5 Short Term Allowances / Per Diem for Associates up to Grade 8 (for trips up to 3 months)

**Countries / Locations and Allowances:**
- **USA/Canada:** $120 per day
- **UK:** £90 per day
- **Europe (Except UK):** €75 per day
- **Australia:** AUD 280 per day
- **UAE:** AED 35 per day
- **Oman:** OMR 80 per day
- **Egypt:** USD 80 per day
- **Asia (except UAE and Oman):** USD 75 per day

**Options for Allowances:**
- **Option 1:** Flat/Fixed Amount (No bills needed)
  - Lodging, Boarding, and Conveyance: Fixed amounts as listed above.
  
- **Option 2:** On Actual Expense (Bills needed to support)
  - **Lodging:** 
    - USA/Canada: $120
    - UK: £110
    - Europe: €120
    - Australia: AUD 400
    - UAE: AED 40
    - Oman: OMR 190
    - Egypt: USD 150
  - **Per Diem Allowance (Boarding & Incidental):**
    - USA/Canada: $60
    - UK: £75
    - Europe: €60
    - Australia: AUD 40
    - UAE: AED 40
    - Oman: OMR 160
    - Egypt: USD 20

- An employee can choose either Option 1 or Option 2, and once chosen, cannot switch options during the trip.

- If Option 1 is selected, the associate must manage all expenses within the fixed amount. If Option 2 is selected, bills for lodging and conveyance must be supported, while no bills are needed for boarding and incidentals.

- Per Diem includes food expenses and all incidental expenses incurred by the associate, such as laundry, health club, entertainment, and parking fines.

### 5.4 Incidental Expenses

- **Incidental Expenses for All Countries**: 
  - Flat rate of **USD 25**.
  
- **Staying with Friend or Relative (Stay allowance without bills)**:
  - **USD 100** for USA, Canada, and Europe (excluding the UK).
  - **USD 250** for the UK.
  - **USD 325** for all other countries.

**Notes**:
- No expenses for lodging, boarding, and incidentals will be reimbursed if on Stay allowance without bills.
- Travel expenses to/from the official client from a place of stay will be reimbursed at actuals within reasonable limits.
- Associates should ensure that staying with friends/relatives does not negatively impact work or working hours.
- The number of travel days will be calculated based on nights stayed with friends or relatives.
- If there is no night stay or the stay is less than 18 hours, boarding expenses can be claimed at actuals, not exceeding an average of **USD 50** per day.
- On multi-location travel, boarding, lodging, and incidental expenses can be claimed except for locations where Stay allowance has been claimed.

### 5.5 No Show Policy

- Associates must report to the airport or train station well in advance of the reporting time for all departures.
- All additional expenses incurred due to delayed reporting and no-shows must be borne by the associate.
- Exceptions apply only in cases of Force Majeure or medical emergencies that could not be communicated in time for ticket cancellation.

### 6.1 Long-term Travel on Work Permit

- Associates traveling for **12 months and above** are entitled to take their family to the nearest airport to their home location, with travel/ticketing services on pre-approval from HR.

### Return Home Policy (Only for UAE)

- Applicable for associates who have completed **13 months** of continuous assignment in UAE and have sufficient leave balance.
- The company will sponsor tickets for associates and their families to the nearest airport to their home location (i.e., Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Delhi airports).
- Vacation travels must be planned well in advance and approved by the Client, Practice Head, and HR.
- If not eligible as per the policy, any trip will be at the associate's own cost, and the company will not sponsor any expenses.

### 6.2 Initial Stay / Settling

- The company will provide guest house/hotel facilities wherever possible for a period of **14 nights**. After this period, associates must arrange for moving to an apartment.
- If associates book their own hotel, accommodation must be within the limits of **USD 100** per night for Indian associates traveling abroad.
- If **14 nights** is insufficient for finding an apartment, any extensions must be approved by the HR Head.
- The company will provide an advance as needed, which must be repaid within one month from the date of travel through the home location.

### 7.1 Expense Report Submission and Review Process
- Associates must reconcile expenses accurately and regularly within 7 days of trip completion.
- Reimbursable and non-reimbursable expenses are subject to audits by tax authorities and clients.
- Clear records of expenses must be maintained.
- Reimbursement of expenses will occur within 15 days from the receipt of original supporting documents, pending manager approval in Fusion.
- Delays may occur if finance has queries regarding the verification of approvals and supporting documents.
- Proper documentation standards must be adhered to; submission of receipts outside the defined process is not permitted and may lead to delays or rejections.

### 7.2 Classification of Expenses
- Reimbursable expenses must be charged to specific engagements or project codes.
- Associates must use the project search feature in Time & Expense to determine the applicable project number.
- Each expense must be categorized as follows:
  - Expenses billable to a client project according to a given statement of work.
  - Expenses charged to a client project but not billable back to the client.
  - Non-billable expenses associated with internal Trianz activities or corporate functions.
- Expense types are available in Fusion, and any changes will be reflected there. A specific expense category must be indicated in the expense report.
