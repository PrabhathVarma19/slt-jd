# Guidelines for Information Labeling and Handling

## 1. Information Classification
- **Public**: Accessible to the public at large.
- **Internal Use Only**: 
  - Read access is granted to all Trianz employees for internal use only.
  - Employees must not share information with any third party or outsourced employees unless authorized by the departmental head.
  - Employees must not share information with anyone outside their own department unless authorized.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Access is limited to authorized individuals only.
  - External access requires a non-disclosure agreement and a business need to know.

## 2. Handling of Information
- Employees must handle information according to its classification, including circulation, maintenance, and discarding.
- Data owners must be identified, and access should be based on the confidentiality of the data.

## 3. Storage of Information
- **Public**: No security control requirements.
- **Internal Use Only**: 
  - Documents should be stored under lock and key.
  - Desktops must be password protected.
  - Hard disks should not be shared at the drive level; shared folders must be restricted to known individuals.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Confidential documents must be stored in the respective user’s folder on the file server and preferably password protected.
  - Confidential documents must not be stored in public directories.
  - The complete hard drive of a desktop must not be shared on the network; only selected folders may be shared with named individuals.
  - Data must be encrypted, and additional controls may be required based on sensitivity.

## 4. Storage of Information Medium
- **Public**: No security control requirements.
- **Internal Use Only**: 
  - Information should be stored in a personal storage shelf, preferably locked.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Information should be kept in a separate fireproof cabinet managed by a person nominated by the department head.
  - Access to the cabinet must be granted by the department head via email.
  - Retrieval of confidential information must be done in the presence of a designated person.
  - Hard copy documents that cannot be reproduced should also be kept in fireproof cabinets.

## 5. Labeling of Information
- **Public**: No specific labeling requirements.
- **Internal Use Only**: 
  - Documents must contain the classification logo indicating ‘Internal Use Only’ at the start of the document/record.
  - Hard copies should have the classification logo on the cover page or title page.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Documents must contain the classification logo indicating ‘Confidential’ at the start of the document/record.
  - Hard copies should have the classification logo on the cover page or title page.

### 6. Labeling of Information

- All email communication containing confidential information must have "Confidential" as the first word in the subject line.
- Data must be labeled according to information asset classification at the time of storage and transfer.
- For email communication, the attached document should have the classification "Internal Use Only" marked on the first page.
- The information medium must be marked "Confidential."
- The information owner is responsible for labeling the document. If there is any doubt regarding classification, the owner must seek concurrence from the departmental head.

### 7. Addressing/Packaging Security Control Requirements

- The storage medium must have two envelopes/layers of packaging.
  - The outer envelope/layer must:
    - Show the recipient's name and address.
    - Be marked "To be opened by addressee only."
    - Display the name and phone number of the sender.
  - The inner envelope/layer should be marked "Confidential."

### 8. Dispatch and Distribution of Information (Except Email)

- Packaging must ensure physical protection of the item.
- Dispatch or distribution of information to anyone outside the department, third party, or outsourced party should be done via:
  - Normal mail service or by hand.
  - Approved courier.
- Printed information sent through internal mail, external mail, or by courier must be sent by trusted courier or registered mail, with a method that provides tracking.
- Internal Use Only documents can be freely circulated only to internal Trianz employees.
- Distribution to named individuals outside Trianz requires approval from the head of the concerned department.
- Confidential information cannot be taken out of Trianz premises in any media (e.g., hard copy, CD, floppy, USB) without prior permission from the department head.
- The document must detail the data retention period based on regulatory, sensitivity, and information classification requirements.
- The validity of data classification must be checked periodically, and data should be reclassified if there are any changes.

### 9. Dispatch and Distribution of Information (Electronic Mail)

- Emails containing information can be sent to internal employees without explicit approval from Trianz authority.
- Circulation of Internal Use Only documents to any third party or anyone outside Trianz requires approval from the head of the concerned department, with a copy marked to them while sending the document.

### Disposal of Information

- No security control requirements are needed for disposal.
- Documents should not be used for draft (reverse side) printing.
- Documents may be destroyed manually, ensuring they cannot be easily reconstructed.
- Circulation of confidential documents to any third party or anyone outside Trianz requires approval from the head of the concerned department via email, with a copy marked to them.
- BCC is not permitted for "Confidential" documents.
- When sending documents to authorized persons, they must be marked "Confidential" in the subject or body of the email in a visible manner.
- Data must be encrypted during transfer using appropriate encryption methodologies.
- Confidential information must not be discussed on speakerphones or during teleconferences unless all parties acknowledge that no unauthorized persons are nearby.
- Information should only be destroyed or deleted after the minimum retention period, when it is obsolete or no longer needed.
- Any hard copy confidential document to be destroyed should be shredded or torn into small pieces at the time of destruction.
- Any CD containing confidential information that cannot be deleted must be physically destroyed.

### 1. Data Disposal Policy

- Confidential information must undergo low-level formatting to ensure that it cannot be recovered from the hard disk.
- The secure disposal of data, including disks, must be documented.
