## 1. Objective
The company utilizes Intranet as a critical resource to carry on the business processes. Therefore, Trianz has developed systems and procedures to ensure that security is maintained for Intranet at all times, with a uniform code of conduct.

## 2. Scope
These guidelines apply to all users of TRIANZ and cover:
- Local Area Network (LAN) and Wide Area Network (WAN) connected with Trianz’s workstations and servers, including applications and system software.
- All systems under the jurisdiction and/or ownership of TRIANZ, including hired systems.
- All workstations and servers supplied by the customers of TRIANZ, as long as they are operated from within TRIANZ premises.

## 4. Rules on Intranet Usage
- All services on the Intranet of Trianz LAN and WAN network are for the purpose of information update and dissemination among employees and selected third-party vendors. All information posted to the Intranet must have a designated owner, and all postings or modifications must be approved.

- **Default Access:**
  - Default Intranet access is granted to all employees based on their department.
  - Default usernames and passwords for Intranet access will be communicated to users.
  - Employees with private protected VLAN/Isolated Wi-Fi connections will also receive default access based on project requirements.
  - All Intranet access will be protected with Zscaler webproxy for security.

- **Access to Other Resources of Intranet:**
  - For access beyond the default, users must raise a Service Request to the IS Department with approval from their respective Head of Department. Access will be granted as per the approval for the specified duration.

- **Authentication & Authorization:**
  - All Intranet access is governed by proper authorization, and access to all intranet servers will be protected and managed through PAM.

- **Information Dissemination:**
  - Intranet information is for dissemination only to authorized individuals. Users must not forward this information to others without seeking approval through the appropriate channel.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
- Any user, as defined in the “Scope section,” is responsible for using TRIANZ’s intranet resources in compliance with this policy.
- The IS Manager is responsible for reviewing these guidelines once a year.
- The designated Information Security Officer/Designated member of the Security Forum, along with the Human Resources group, shall be responsible for distributing these guidelines.
