## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the Pandemic Response Plan is to guide Trianz through the development of the steps and procedures necessary to successfully respond to and recover from pandemics.

## 2. Scope
The guidelines and procedures listed in this plan cover a variety of business activities, including sensitive or critical daily operational activities, and apply to all company personnel.

## 3. Objectives of Trianz Pandemic Plan
The objectives of this pandemic plan are to:
- Limit the impact of the pandemic on Trianz by reducing morbidity and mortality.
- Minimize social disruption caused by the pandemic.
- Reduce economic losses attributable to the pandemic.
- Mitigate risks of delivery impact to clients.

## 4. Definition of a Pandemic
A pandemic is defined as a virulent global outbreak to which humans have little or no immunity, capable of mutating and transmitting easily between people, spreading worldwide. The severity of viruses can range from mild to severe, and their effects can vary significantly across different age groups or at-risk populations.

## 5. Trianz Pandemic Response Objectives
The objectives of the Trianz Pandemic Response are to:
- Detect and identify early symptoms through employee wellness surveys.
- Diagnose by enabling medical care from Trianz empanelled doctors and clinics.
- Contain the situation by enabling remote working and providing business continuity for client delivery.
- Monitor the health of associates and assist as needed, along with tracking project delivery status.
- Communicate the overall status to key stakeholders, including internal teams, clients, suppliers, and vendors.

## 6. Plan Triggers and Actions
Trianz has established triggers to activate pandemic-related strategies, recognizing the need for immediate action if employee health or client delivery is impacted.

### Alert Phase
- **Trigger:** New strain identified
  - **Key Actions:**
    - Establish situational awareness.
    - Crisis Management team to publish a situation assessment report to the Business Resilience Group.
    - Assess potential impacts on business operations and/or clients.
    - Evaluate transportation and travel risks.
    - Review/update human resource-related policies and procedures.
    - Review business continuity planning efforts for personnel and third parties.
    - Evaluate IT's ability to support a spike in remote work.
    - Determine communication requirements (internal and external).

### Pre-Pandemic Phase
- **Trigger:** Presence of strain in localized hot spots
  - **Key Actions:**
    - Continuous crisis management response.
    - Initiate health surveys and assess the health status of associates.
    - Accelerate business continuity planning checkpoints for projects and functions likely to be affected.
    - Re-evaluate continuity strategies for potentially affected third parties.
    - Establish communication with financial stakeholders.

### 6.0 Pandemic Phase Key Actions

#### Phase Trigger: Global/Dispersed Pandemic
- Activate Crisis Management and Appropriate Business Continuity Proliferation of Pandemic Response.
- Continuous Business Continuity Plan (BCP) checkpoints.
- Continuous health trend monitoring via surveys.

#### Phase Trigger: Higher than Seasonal Average Absenteeism
- Continuous delivery status monitoring via daily delivery status reviews.
- Regular client communication and updates.
- Continue to impose travel restrictions.
- Activate additional sanitation measures if required.
- Activate more aggressive strategies.
- Activate alternate supply-chain and sourcing strategies.
- Establish direct lines of communication with local public health agencies.
- Execute mass communications.
- Initiate vaccination initiatives and collaborate with hospitals.
- Evaluate the need for medicinal supplies and life support accessories such as oxygen cylinders and concentrators.

#### Transition Phase
- Conduct a post-incident review.
- Update plans and procedures.
- Transition to normal operations when employee availability returns to normal levels.
- Communicate to key stakeholders.
- Implement continuous vaccination programs.

### 7.0 Pandemic Preparedness and Response Measures

#### People
- Distribute HR awareness mailers highlighting pandemic outbreaks along with Dos and Don’ts.
- Collaborate with empanelled clinics across all Trianz locations for doctors’ advice.
- Provide a 24/7 help desk facility directing associates to medical facilities.
- Conduct an Employee Wellness Survey to assess medical assistance needs for employees and their families.
- Establish location-wise HR, Admin, and IT points of contact with reach-out numbers.

#### Environment Readiness
- Conduct location-wise fumigation and disinfecting of delivery centers.
- Make hand sanitizers available at security desks, washrooms, and prominent places inside delivery centers.
- Keep face masks readily available.
- Screen for symptoms at security desks.
- Utilize thermal sensors for screening.
- Housekeeping services to screen for symptoms.
- Screen cab drivers for symptoms.
- Work with building owners for environment cleansing.

#### Travel and Local Commute
- Implement a freeze on domestic and international travel.
- Provide travel advisory and diagnostics to assess travel risks to pandemic outbreak countries or cities.
- Enforce self-quarantine for travel from or transit through pandemic outbreak countries.
- Administer diagnostic questions to visitors and vendors to assess risks.

#### Remote Working & IT Enablement
- Enable Work From Home (WFH) by proactively assessing and allocating laptops, internet connectivity, and power backups for critical associates.
- Conduct daily connects (Daily Status Reports) with delivery managers to check on wellbeing and delivery status.
- Ensure primary and secondary data connectivity and power wherever feasible.

#### Cyber Security, Technology & Delivery Aspects
- Implement remote working guidelines and best practices.
- Roll out and implement cybersecurity guidelines.
- Secure remote workspace with hardened laptops, protected web and email access, and conditional access.
- Conduct daily delivery risk reviews across all projects.
- Provide information security and privacy refresher training and awareness campaigns.
- Hold daily BCP checkpoint calls to assess:
  - Employee health and wellbeing.
  - Endpoint device and connectivity issues.
  - Delivery status and risk monitoring.
  - Client status updates and briefings.

### 9. Business Continuity Strategies

#### 9.1 Work from Home Model (Remote Working)
- The Work from Home model is activated when primary or secondary facilities are inoperable due to a pandemic or epidemic.
- Associates will work from a remote location using Trianz assets and equipment.

##### 9.1.1 Information Technology Assets
- Trianz will provide official laptops, headphones, and other peripherals.
- Connectivity to the Trianz network or email requires IS configuration.
- Requests for equipment must be authorized by the Project/Delivery Manager.

##### 9.1.2 Power and Network Connectivity
- Associates must have continuous power and sufficient power backups.
- Trianz provides power backups like UPS to identified critical employees.
- All associates must have sufficient broadband internet connectivity and backup internet dongles provided by the company.

##### 9.1.3 Security Requirements
- Identified associates must complete all mandatory security training (Information Security, Data Privacy, etc.) before working from home.
- Associates must adhere to remote cybersecurity guidelines published by the Information Security team.
- All work from home users must comply with policies specified under the Information Security Management System in the Bluebook.

### 10. Hybrid Model
- The hybrid working model allows employees to work remotely while others work on-premises with suitable preventive controls.
- This model provides structure and sociability in an office environment while offering flexibility and independence of remote working.

### 11. Pandemic Response Plan Test
- The pandemic response plan test shall be conducted as per management guidelines at the organizational or engagement level.
- Testing may include simulations, tabletop exercises, or plan walkthroughs aligned with the Trianz Business Continuity Plan.
