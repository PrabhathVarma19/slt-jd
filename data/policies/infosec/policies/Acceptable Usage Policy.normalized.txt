## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the acceptable use of equipment and computing services.

## 2. Objective
The objective of this policy is to describe the acceptable use of information technology equipment and services at Trianz.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Trianz managed business systems, Trianz provided Client Services, and Trianz developed products.

## 4. Policy Statement
- Activities that are illegal under local, state, federal, or international law while utilizing TRIANZ owned resources are strictly prohibited for all employees, contractors, or third-party service providers.
- This policy applies to all stakeholders working for Trianz products and services.

## 5. Acceptable Use
- Associates may install necessary utilities like software or applications only after receiving approval from IS and InfoSec & Data Privacy Assurance.
- All associates must respect and protect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of Trianz information and resources.
- All associates/users must respect and protect the intellectual property rights of others by following copyright laws.
- Users must take ownership and report all security-related incidents using the designated tool.
- Users must use Trianz assets for official purposes only.
- Associates may use their mobile phones to access Outlook, VPN tokens, etc., securely and in adherence to the MDM policy.
- Associates may use personal laptops for official use in compliance with the BYOD policy.
- Associates may browse safe internet sites in accordance with the internet user policy.

## 6. Unacceptable Use

### 6.1 System and Network Activities
- Violating the rights of any person or company protected by copyright, trade secret, patent, or similar laws, including the installation or distribution of unlicensed software, is prohibited.
- Unauthorized copying of copyrighted material, including digitization and distribution of photographs, music, or software without an active license, is strictly prohibited.
- Exporting software or technical information in violation of international or regional export control laws is illegal; consult management before exporting any questionable material.
- Introducing malicious programs into the network or server (e.g., viruses, worms, Trojan Horses) is prohibited.
- Sharing account/access credentials or allowing others to use your account is not allowed.
- Using TRIANZ computing assets to procure offensive content or transmit material violating POSH or hostile workplace laws is prohibited.
- Making fraudulent offers of products or services from any TRIANZ account is not allowed.
- Making statements about warranty or commitment, unless part of normal job duties, is prohibited.
- Any action causing disruptions to network communication or unauthorized access is not allowed.
- Port scanning or security scanning is prohibited unless prior notification is made to the IT department.
- Executing network monitoring that intercepts data not intended for the employee's host is prohibited unless part of normal job duties.
- Circumventing user authentication or security of any host, network, or account is not allowed.
- Interfering with or denying service to any user other than the employee's host is prohibited.

### 6.1 Prohibited Activities
- **Interference with User Sessions**: It is prohibited to use any program, script, command, or send messages with the intent to interfere with or disable a user's terminal session, whether locally or via the Internet/Intranet/Extranet.
- **Disclosure of Employee Information**: Providing information about or lists of TRIANZ employees to parties outside TRIANZ is prohibited.
- **Cloud Computing Services**: 
  - The use of cloud computing services for work purposes must be formally authorized by the IT Manager or CIO.
  - The IT Manager/CIO is responsible for ensuring that security, privacy, and all other IT management requirements are adequately addressed by the cloud computing vendor.
  - Any cloud services requiring users to agree to terms of service must have those agreements reviewed and approved by the CISO or CIO.
  - The use of cloud services must comply with cloud security and data privacy controls governing the handling of personally identifiable information, corporate financial data, or any other data owned or collected by TRIANZ.
  - Personal cloud service accounts may not be used for the storage, manipulation, or exchange of company-related communications or company-owned data.

### 6.2 Email and Communications Activities
- **Unsolicited Emails**: Sending unsolicited email messages, including "junk mail" or advertising material to individuals who did not specifically request such material, is prohibited.
- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment via email, telephone, or paging, whether through language, frequency, or size of messages, is prohibited.
- **Email Forgery**: Unauthorized use or forging of email header information is prohibited.
- **Solicitation for Email Addresses**: Soliciting email for any other email address, other than that of the poster's account, with the intent to harass or collect replies, is prohibited.
- **Chain Letters and Schemes**: Creating or forwarding "chain letters," "Ponzi," or other "pyramid" schemes of any type is prohibited.
- **Unsolicited Email for Advertising**: The use of unsolicited email originating from within TRIANZ's networks or other Internet/Intranet/Extranet service providers to advertise any service hosted by TRIANZ or connected via TRIANZâ€™s network is prohibited.
- **Newsgroup Spam**: Posting the same or similar non-business-related messages to large numbers of Usenet newsgroups (newsgroup spam) is prohibited.

### 7. Exceptions
- **No Exceptions**: There are no exceptions to the above rules.

### 8. ISO Control Mapping
- **Category of Control**: Organizational controls
- **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 5.10 Acceptable use of information and other associated assets
- **Control Rules**: The acceptable use and procedures for handling information and other associated assets shall be identified, documented, and implemented.
