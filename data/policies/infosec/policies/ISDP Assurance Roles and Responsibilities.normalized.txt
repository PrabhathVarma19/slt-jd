## 1. Introduction
The ISDP Assurance Team enables Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance across Trianz and ensures compliance with various ISO Standards and Regulations.

### 1.1 Objectives
- Ensure that the organization’s Information Security & Privacy Management System is established, implemented, maintained, and improved.
- Demonstrate the organization’s commitment to securing organizational and client information assets against vulnerabilities and threats.

## 3. Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities

### 3.1 Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Holds overall responsibility for Information/Cyber Security and Privacy for Trianz.
- Sets clear vision, mission, and direction for the Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance Team.
- Communicates the cybersecurity posture and any risks to stakeholders both internally and externally on a periodic basis.

### 3.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Ensures that ISMS policies and procedures are defined and implemented throughout the organization.
- Updates information security policies and recommended practices in a timely manner to reflect current modifications.
- Reviews the information security policy at least annually.
- Provides information security awareness to all personnel in the organization, as applicable.
- Identifies and approves security requirements for new information processing facilities.
- Defines appropriate technical architecture for IT infrastructure security and monitors compliance.
- Arranges necessary resources and skills for conducting periodic information security reviews.
- Assesses and ensures disaster recovery and business continuity preparedness for IT systems.
- Defines appropriate security controls for all applications in consultation with application owners.
- Maintains risk assessment documentation and a list of identified vulnerabilities.
- Reviews all critical incidents and their resolution timeframes.
- Conducts ongoing risk assessments of Trianz Information Assets.
- Carries out quarterly Vulnerability Assessment Tests of Trianz network and Cloud infrastructure.
- Involves in-house or external security specialists as needed for specific information security requirements.
- Decides on the applicability of disciplinary policy for reported security incidents.
- Provides directives for ensuring organization-wide information security.
- Delegates executive responsibilities for ensuring Information Security & Privacy.
- Takes necessary measures to maintain the security of information systems.
- Ensures staff is adequately trained to meet security and privacy requirements.

### 3.2 Requirements of the Organization
- Responsibilities will be defined, and procedures will be established to promptly detect, investigate, report, and resolve information security incidents.
- Provisions will be in place for the continued protection of information systems resources within the organization.
- Authority is granted to approve vendors for selecting appropriate information security products.
- Root Cause Analysis (RCA) will be conducted for information security incidents and exceptions.
- All Information Security Management System (ISMS) and Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) related policies and process documents require approval.

### 3.3 Information Security Group
The responsibilities of the Information Security Compliance Group and its team members include:
- Ensuring that ISMS functions within their respective business groups comply with Trianz Information Security Policy and Data Privacy Policies and Procedures.
- Maintaining data classification according to Trianz's classification labeling and handling standards.
- Monitoring adherence to Trianz Information Security and Data Privacy Policies and Procedures.
- Reporting any information security incidents and data privacy breaches to the Information Security Assurance Council and relevant supervisory authorities, such as reporting security breaches to CERT-in within 6 hours.
- Coordinating with other members of the Information Security Assurance Council for the continuous implementation of ISMS and PIMS.
- Being accountable on behalf of their respective groups for ISMS and PIMS.
- Providing suggestions to the Information Security Assurance Council for enhancing information security and data privacy measures.
- Ensuring that information security procedures relevant to business groups align with Trianz Information Security Policy and Data Privacy Policy.
- Mapping contractual obligations to the Master Service Agreement (MSA) and providing walkthroughs to project teams.
- Ensuring third-party risk and compliance with vendors.

### 3.4 Data Privacy Assurance Group
#### Procedure
- Follow the Security Incident Response Procedure for containment, eradication, etc.

#### Breach Notification Procedure
- If Trianz is a Processor, the Data Privacy Representative (DPR) will notify the Controller of data privacy breaches within 48 hours of the breach and follow further actions as directed by the Controller (Client).
- If Trianz is a Controller, the DPR/CISO will notify the Supervisory Authority of data privacy breaches within 72 hours of the incident.
- If Trianz is a Controller, the DPR/CISO will notify CERT-In of data privacy breaches within 6 hours of identification, based on the incident's criticality.
- The DPR/CISO will notify data subjects as advised by the Supervisory Authority and as required by data privacy regulations.

#### Consent Procedure
- Ensure that the consent procedure is followed for processing personal and sensitive data.

#### Cross Border Data Transfer Procedure
- Assist the project delivery/support team in ensuring compliance with cross-border data regulations.
- Review data portability requests from all functions and maintain a central register.
- Address data portability requests within 30 days.
- If data cannot be shared within 30 days, update the data subject within that timeframe and document the communication.
- Ensure data is shared within 90 days of the request from the data subject.

#### Data Privacy Impact Assessment (DPIA) Procedure
- Ensure the availability of Data Privacy Diagnostic or threshold assessments where applicable at project initiation.
- Support the Project Manager and Delivery Manager in conducting DPIAs or PIAs where applicable.
- Assist in identifying risks, prioritizing them, and developing risk mitigation plans, including tracking and documenting their effectiveness.

#### Data Subject Rights Procedure
- Conduct an annual review of Data Subjects' Rights Procedures for adequacy.
- Perform a quarterly review of the Data Subject Rights log for compliance with regulations and implement corrective actions as needed.

#### Withdrawal of Consent Procedure
- Periodic review of privacy by design procedures by the Information Security Assurance Council and DPR.

### 3.4 Modification/Withdrawal of Consent Forms and Status of Processing of Personal Data

- Support the implementation of Privacy by Design and Default by identifying data protection requirements.
- Incorporate applicable geography-related data protection requirements into internal processes.
- Establish a procedure for Privacy Notices.

### Retention of Records Procedure

- Review the Retention of Records Procedure annually.

### Information Security Governance Council

- Conduct a yearly review of privacy concerns of data subjects and implement corrective actions.
- Review the Privacy Notice and sample privacy consent notice for adequacy.

### Audits & Compliance

- Facilitate internal and external audits and compliance with respect to privacy/security assessments, certifications, or seals.
- Support privacy/security assessments by clients.
- Ensure project compliance with various security standards and regulations, such as ISO 27701 and SOC.
- Ensure organizational compliance with applicable privacy laws, including GDPR and CCPA.
- Document and present internal review and audit findings to leadership.

### Policies and Procedures

- Maintain and ensure compliance with the Data Privacy Management System (DPMS) / Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) for privacy policies, procedures, and templates.

### Risk and Privacy Impact Assessment

- Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) to identify privacy risks and manage them for IT services projects and corporate functions.
- Ensure third-party risk management for privacy/security by supporting the contract management process and conducting privacy risk assessments of vendors.
- Assess internal applications for any privacy/security risks and facilitate privacy risk management.

### Privacy Trainings

- Roll out required amendments of Data Privacy Trainings to the organization.
- Continually learn, share knowledge, and foster exchanges of skills.
- Proactively identify opportunities to improve the quality of reporting and usability of information.
- Implement applicable privacy laws for any amendments or new business geographies related to privacy laws.
- Interact with internal and external security and privacy professionals as needed.

### Maintenance of Personal Data Inventory

- Maintain a Personal Data Inventory for corporate functions in the role of Data Controller.

### 3.5 Audits

- Ensure yearly planning for internal and external audits.
- Conduct bi-annual internal audits and yearly external audits for Quality Management System, Information Security Management System, Privacy Information Management System, and Service Management System across Trianz locations in India, US-Virginia, and US-California.
- Ensure end-to-end internal audit planning, implementation, auditor selection, training, and closure of audit findings.
- Conduct internal and external audits for products.
- Ensure SOC audits for projects and functions across Trianz.

### 3.6 Business Continuity

- Establish a Business Resilience and Crisis Management structure across all locations to facilitate local resumption of activities and deployment of recovery resources.
- Design and establish a Business Continuity Management (BCM) framework for the company, including subsidiary operations, to guide business continuity efforts.
- Conduct business impact analysis and BCP risk assessments for Trianz offices and critical functions, developing continuity plans accordingly.
- Regularly test business continuity plans for key engagements and IT systems, reporting results to management.
- Periodically review and update business continuity plans in response to changes.
- Develop and provide staff training on BCM through awareness sessions, eLearning, and mailers.
- Collaborate with delivery and corporate functions to develop and implement best practices for data and system protection and restoration in the event of natural disasters and security/privacy incidents.
