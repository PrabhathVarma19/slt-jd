## 1. Scope
- The purpose of this ISMS Manual is to outline the Information Security Management System (ISMS) implemented at Trianz, which complies with ISO 27001:2022 and ISO 27701:2019 Standards.

### 6. Planning

#### 6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities

##### 6.1.1 General
- Trianz shall consider issues and determine risks and opportunities when planning for the Information Security Management System (ISMS) to:
  - Achieve intended outcomes related to Confidentiality (C), Integrity (I), Availability (A), and Privacy (P) of information assets.
  - Ensure transparency in operations.
  - Meet customer information security needs and maintain Business Continuity Planning (BCP) strategies.
  - Reduce undesired effects such as breaches of information security requirements, data loss, or integrity issues.
  - Achieve continual improvement through stakeholder feedback, customer feedback, internal audit results, and objective accomplishments.
- Risk mitigations will be implemented to counter negative effects, determined through risk assessment methodologies.
- Refer to the Risk & Opportunity Management Procedure and the Business Continuity and Disaster Procedure for further details.

##### 6.1.2 Information Security and Privacy Risk Assessment
- During risk assessment, the risk value of an asset is calculated as the product of Business Impact and Probability of Occurrence.
- The values for Confidentiality (C), Integrity (I), Availability (A), and Privacy (P) are rated on a scale of 1, 3, and 9.
- Asset identification will be conducted for all assets containing information value in each department.
- A unified risk assessment methodology will be developed to identify risks.
- Risk owners will be identified during the assessment, and all applicable threats and vulnerabilities for each asset will be documented.
- The risk assessment methodology will be used for all identified assets.
- Refer to the Risk and Opportunity Management Procedure in the ISMS Procedure folder for more information.

##### 6.1.3 Information Security Risk Treatment
- Based on the risk assessment, Delivery and Corporate Functions/Support Functions shall develop a Risk Treatment Plan.
- The CIO/CISO has the authority to recognize and accept risks when no feasible or cost-effective risk treatment plan can be implemented.
- The Risk Treatment Plan will be implemented to achieve identified control objectives, considering funding and allocation of roles and responsibilities.
- For more information, refer to the Risk and Assessment and Opportunity Management Procedure in the ISMS Procedure folder.

##### 6.1.4 Information Security and Privacy Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them
- Top Management shall establish and direct the ISDPA team to ensure that information security objectives are defined at relevant functions and levels in line with the information security policy.
- These objectives must be measurable and ensure that information security requirements are met, considering results from risk assessments and treatment history.
- Information Security Objectives shall be documented, detailing the plan to achieve them, including responsibilities, required resources, and evaluation mechanisms.
- The ISDPA will ensure communication with all relevant stakeholders and will review and monitor the Information Security Objectives.

### 7.5 Documented Information

#### 7.5.1 General
There are two types of documents at a high level:
- **Blue Book Documents**: This includes Policies, Manuals, Group Manuals, Procedures, Guidelines, Checklists, and Templates.
- **Documents related to Projects and Corporate Functions**:
  - Documents prepared during project execution, such as PM Workbooks, SRS, Design, Test Plan, Test Cases, etc.
  - Documents related to Corporate Functions, including InfoSec and Compliance, HR, Recruitment, L&D, Admin, Purchase, and IS Operations.

#### 7.5.2 Creating and Updating
- Records are identified in each procedure and maintained by the process owners for the purpose of the Information Security Management System (ISMS).
- These records serve as evidence for implementing the ISMS and for checking its effectiveness periodically.
- The internal audit team verifies these records against the procedures to ensure adequacy.
- Most records are maintained in either soft copy or hard copy format.
- Backup processes ensure availability of copies, and the access control policy ensures confidentiality.

#### 7.5.3 Control of Documented Information
- All Blue Book documents are controlled through proper reviews and approvals.
- Changes to controlled documents must be reviewed and approved by the appropriate authority before release for use.
- All evolving documents are version controlled, and only the relevant approved versions are available for use.
- All documents are accessible online for reference by authorized personnel in a common database.
- Controlled documents are identified by a unique Release Identification (Release ID).
- Documents of external origin are suitably identified and made available only for intended purposes and users.
- Any Blue Book process document used for proposals, customer offsite usage, or any distribution outside the organization is considered uncontrolled, and change control does not apply once it leaves Trianz premises.
- Blue Book Process Artifacts that are no longer in use are moved to an obsolete folder.
- All records identified by projects and corporate functions are generated and stored as specific records.
- Records related to projects and corporate functions are retained for 7 years from the date of creation. Records older than 7 years will be disposed of at the discretion of the Project Manager or Corporate Function Manager.
- All ISMS documents and records will be protected and controlled as per the Documented Information Procedure, available in the Common Support Process Folder in the Blue Book.

### 9.4 Management Review

1. Top management shall review the organization’s information security management system (ISMS) at planned intervals to ensure its continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness.
2. The management review shall include consideration of:
   - The status of actions from previous management reviews.
   - Changes in external and internal issues relevant to the ISMS.
   - Changes in needs and expectations of interested parties relevant to the ISMS.
   - Feedback on information security performance, including trends in:
     - Nonconformities and corrective actions.
     - Monitoring and measurement results.
     - Audit results.
     - Fulfillment of information security objectives.
   - Feedback from interested parties.
   - Results of risk assessment and status of the risk treatment plan.
   - Opportunities for continual improvement.
3. The outputs of the management review shall include decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to the ISMS.
4. Documented information shall be retained as evidence of the results of the management review.
5. The effectiveness of the ISMS is reviewed periodically, typically with risk assessments conducted every six months and management reviews conducted annually.
6. Reviews will assess the level of residual risk and acceptable risk, considering:
   - Organizational changes such as changes in processes and IT infrastructure.
   - Technology adopted for service delivery.
   - Business objectives and processes required for service delivery.
   - Identified threats during ISMS operation over time.
   - External events, including changes to the legal or regulatory environment and social climate, as well as contractual obligations related to information security.
7. The need for reassessment of risks will be evaluated during the management review.

### 10. Nonconformity and Corrective Action

1. The ISAC team at Trianz will take action to eliminate the causes of nonconformities associated with the implementation and operation of the ISMS to prevent recurrence.
2. Documented procedures for corrective action will define requirements for:
   - Identifying nonconformities in the implementation and/or operation of the ISMS.
   - Determining the causes of nonconformities and potential impacts associated with damage to the ISMS.
   - Evaluating the need for actions to ensure that nonconformities are not repeated.
   - Determining and implementing the necessary corrective actions.
   - Recording the results of actions taken.

### 10.1 Reviewing of Corrective Action Taken
- Potential nonconformities (NCs) are identified and eliminated to prevent their occurrence.

### 10.2 Continual Improvement
- The organization has defined and documented an Information Security policy and objectives. The ISMS Measurement Framework is designed with these policy and objectives in mind.
- A Top Management Review will be conducted to assess the implementation and effectiveness of the ISMS, leading to necessary corrective or preventive actions for identified improvements.
- Internal audits, customer audits, and certification body audits are conducted periodically to ensure adherence to the Blue Book and to promote continual improvement.
- Corrective and preventive actions are taken to prevent the recurrence and occurrence of nonconformities.
- Lessons learned from the security experiences of other organizations, including clients, vendors, suppliers, partners, and advisors, are applied.
- Employees are encouraged to initiate process improvements by submitting a ‘process change request’ to the Trianz Process Improvement Group (TPIG).
- The Infosec team evaluates process change requests and implements them in the Blue Book if deemed feasible.

### Corrective Action
- The organization takes action to eliminate the causes of nonconformities to prevent recurrence.
- After identifying nonconformities, a root cause analysis (RCA) is conducted to identify root causes and corrective actions.
- Identified corrective actions are implemented, and their effectiveness is verified.
- If a corrective action requires a process change, the appropriate Blue Book process artifact will be modified.
