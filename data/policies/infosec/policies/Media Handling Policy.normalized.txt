## 1. Purpose
Improper handling of removable media can cause data loss and leakage. This policy outlines the media handling requirements to ensure confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of information.

## 2. Objective
To ensure the secure management of media to protect sensitive or personal information from intentional or accidental exposure or misuse.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Management, Employees, Contractors, and Third-Party Employees who use Trianz media, as well as all products, services, and organizational IT assets of Trianz.

## 4. Privacy
The media handling policy document is considered "confidential" and shall be made available to authorized personnel with proper access control. Changes and versions of the document shall be controlled.

## 5. Policy Statements

### 5.1 Media Handling
- All Trianz associates are responsible for ensuring the physical security of media.
- Use of USB flash drives is blocked for all associates; approval from CIO/CISO is required for use.
- Personal CDs, DVDs, data tapes, hard drives, flash drives, external memory cards, and other devices are not allowed.
- Use of CD/DVD writers is restricted; printer facilities are for official use only.
- Media must be handled with care, kept away from magnetic materials, and not exposed to extreme heat or pollution.
- Compliance with this policy is the responsibility of overall delivery and corporate functions.

### 5.2 Media Shipping for Work-from-home Associates
- All devices containing information must be shipped securely, following special procedures when associates work from home.
- Shipping must be done from Trianz office or to/from non-office locations.
- Special controls should protect sensitive information during transit from unauthorized disclosure or modification.
- A record must be maintained for incoming and outgoing physical media containing Personally Identifiable Information (PII) or Personal Sensitive Data.
- Packaging for media in transit must adequately protect contents from physical damage according to manufacturersâ€™ specifications.

### 5.3 Disposal of Media and Equipment
- Media must be disposed of securely and safely when no longer required to prevent sensitive information leakage.
- Reusable media must be low-level formatted before removal from the company, with prior approval from CISO/CIO.
- Disposal of sensitive items must be logged to maintain an audit trail and meet regulatory requirements.
- Equipment containing storage media must be checked for confidential information, which should be backed up or removed prior to disposal.
- Storage devices with sensitive information must be physically destroyed or securely overwritten; low-level formatting is preferred over standard deletion.
- All licensed software on equipment must be removed before disposal.
- Damaged storage devices with sensitive data must be assessed for data access before deciding to repair, discard, or destroy.

### 5.4 Removal of Equipment
- Equipment such as desktops and laptops must be handled according to the established procedures for removal.

### 5.4 Off-Site Information Handling
- Information or software will not be taken off-site without prior authorization from IS Operations and the CISO/CIO.
- A Verification Report must be prepared by an authorized IT representative and sent, either physically signed or via email, to the admin for preparing a gate pass.

### 5.5 Reuse of Equipment
- Equipment shall be reused where feasible. Data and software will be securely removed, and the equipment will be re-provisioned for use.
- Parts may be dismantled and reused if there is potential for recovering valuable components from the item.
- Equipment will be recycled at the unit or component level using the external disposal contract coordinated by IT Services.

### 6. Roles & Responsibilities
- **All Associates**: Any exceptions to this policy must be requested with proper business justification and approved by the reporting Manager and CISO/CIO.
- **Admin Team**: Ensure all logistics are in line with this policy and related procedures.
- **IS Operation Team**: Ensure all IS operations comply with this policy and related procedures.
- **Infosec Team (CISO/CIO)**: Approve exceptions based on business justifications and the risk of information loss.
