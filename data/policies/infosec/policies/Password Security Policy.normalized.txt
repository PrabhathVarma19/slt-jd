## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standard for the creation of strong passwords, protection of those passwords, and the frequency of change.

## 2. Objective
Ensure a standard for the creation of strong passwords, the protection of those passwords, and the frequency of change of the passwords.

## 3. Scope
This policy covers all employees, including contract employees who are responsible for one or more accounts (e.g., Email, Server accounts, System accounts, etc.) or have access to any resource that requires a password. The policy applies to all stakeholders of internal Trianz products and services.

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Password Construction Requirements
- Passwords must be a minimum of eight characters in length.
- Passwords must consist of at least three of the following four character sets:
  - Lowercase alpha characters (e.g., a, b, c, d, e)
  - Uppercase alpha characters (e.g., A, B, C, D, E)
  - Numbers (e.g., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5)
  - Special symbols or punctuation characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, %, &, *, _, +, ~, ., >)
- Passwords must not be the same as the User ID.
- Passwords must expire within a maximum of 42 days but not “0”.
- The 'Minimum password age' must be set to 1 or more days but not “0”.
- Passwords must not be identical to the previous eight passwords.
- Passwords must not be transmitted in clear or plain text outside Trianz.
- Passwords must not be visible while typing.
- Passwords must not contain series of the same letters.
- Password resets must be done only on the authorized user’s request.

### 4.2 Password Creation
- All user-level and system-level passwords must comply with the Password Construction Requirements outlined in section 4.1.
- Users must not include personal information such as name, phone number, social security number, date of birth, or addresses in passwords.
- User accounts with system-level privileges granted through group memberships or programs (e.g., Sudo) must have a unique password from all other accounts held by that user.

### 4.3 Password Change
- All system-level passwords (e.g., root, NT admin, application administration accounts) must be dual controlled and changed at least once every 42 days but not “0”.
- All user-level passwords (e.g., Email, web, desktop computer) must be changed at least once every 42 days but not “0”.
- All password change procedures must include:
  - Authentication of the user prior to changing the password (acceptable forms of authentication include answering a series of questions).
  - The new password must comply with password strength requirements associated with the data classification for the service in question.

### 4.4 Password Protection
- Users are responsible for the security of their passwords and are accountable for any loss or compromise.
- Passwords must not be shared with anyone and must be treated as sensitive, confidential Trianz information.
- Any user suspecting that their password may have been compromised must change the password immediately and report the incident to is@trianz.com and copy itservicedesk@trianz.com.
- Passwords must not be inserted into email messages or other forms of electronic communication.
- Passwords should not be revealed to anyone (including administrative assistants, secretaries, managers, co-workers while on vacation, and family members) over the phone or on questionnaires.
- Users should not write passwords down and store them anywhere in their office, on paper, computer systems, or mobile devices (phone, tablet) without encryption.
- Users should not use the "Remember Password" feature of applications (e.g., web browsers).
- Passwords shall be locked after five unsuccessful attempts.
- Vendor or service account default passwords must be removed from computer systems prior to deployment, and new passwords must be implemented on all systems immediately upon installation.

### 4.5 Roles and Responsibilities

- **All Associates**: 
  - Responsible for changing the password as per the policy.
  - Ensure the security of the password.

- **IS Operations Team**: 
  - Responsible for the deployment of the Password Policy.
  - Reset the password based on requests from users.

- **Product/Project Manager**: 
  - Publishes and maintains policy guidelines for the creation, safeguarding, and control of passwords.

- **InfoSec & Compliance Team**: 
  - Conducts audits of password policy adherence during internal audits.
