## 1. Purpose
To provide management with a method to address non-conformances to basic work-related rules and regulations for effective functioning of Trianz, including Attendance, Work performance, Violation of policies, or other work-related issues, and to improve conformance by taking appropriate actions permissible under this policy.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees. The disciplinary process shall not be initiated without prior verification that an information security policy violation has occurred.

## 3. Objective
- Encourage all employees to achieve and maintain Trianz standards of conduct.
- Ensure that all alleged failures of discipline are handled fairly, reasonably, and consistently.

## 4. Policy
Employees who engage in any of the following activities, as well as violations of any Trianz policies, will be subject to disciplinary action, including dismissal. The disciplinary process shall not be initiated without prior verification of an information security policy violation.

The formal disciplinary process will consider:
- The nature (who, what, when, how) and gravity of the breach and its consequences.
- Whether the offense was intentional (malicious) or unintentional (accidental).
- Whether this is a first or repeated offense.
- Whether the violator was properly trained.

The response will also consider relevant legal, statutory, regulatory, contractual, and business requirements.

The disciplinary process serves as a deterrent against violations of the information security policy and related procedures. Deliberate violations may require immediate action.

### 4.1 Information Security and Data Privacy
- Noncompliance with the organization’s Information Security Policy and Data Privacy Policy, as well as Client-defined Security Policy, may result in HR consequences, including warnings and termination.

### 4.2 Attendance
- Habitual late coming, irregular attendance, or absconding is prohibited.
- Absence without leave or overstaying sanctioned leave for more than ten consecutive days without sufficient grounds or satisfactory explanation is prohibited.
- Absence from the employee’s appointed place of work without permission or sufficient cause is prohibited.

### 4.3 Safety & Health
- Violating safety regulations is prohibited.
- Creating or contributing to unsanitary conditions is prohibited.
- Possession, consumption, or being under the influence of intoxicants or narcotics on company premises is prohibited and may lead to disciplinary action, including termination.
- Interference or tampering with safety devices installed on company premises is prohibited.
- Improper use of office equipment is prohibited.
- Possessing firearms, weapons, explosives, etc., on company premises is prohibited.

### 4.4 Misuse of Property
- Damage to or improper use of company property, either willfully or through gross negligence, is prohibited.
- Unauthorized possession of company property is prohibited.
- Disclosure of confidential company information to outsiders without proper authorization is prohibited.
- Unauthorized use of bulletin boards and/or posting notices in unauthorized places is prohibited.
- Stealing or committing any criminal offense on company property is prohibited.
- Introducing or causing to introduce any computer contaminant or virus into any computer, system, or network is prohibited.
- Damaging or causing damage to any computer, system, network, data, or programs is prohibited.
- Disrupting or causing disruption of any computer, system, or network is prohibited.
- Denying or causing the denial of access to any authorized person to any computer, system, or network is prohibited.

### 4.5 Misconduct

The following actions are considered misconduct and are prohibited:

- **Physical Assault**: Engaging in fighting, hitting, pushing, forcibly grabbing another employee, client, contractor, or business associate, or committing similar physical acts or threats while on company premises or in relation to company business.
- **Disorderly Conduct**: Exhibiting drunkenness, riotous, disorderly, or indecent behavior on company premises or outside, if related to employment.
- **Sleeping on Duty**: Sleeping while on duty is prohibited.
- **Abusive Language**: Using abusive or threatening language is not allowed.
- **Harassment**: Any act of harassment (sexual, racial, or otherwise), including telling sexist or racial jokes and making racial or ethnic slurs, is prohibited.
- **Indecent Conduct**: There is a zero tolerance policy for indecent, disorderly, offensive, or immoral conduct.
- **Criminal Activity**: Committing a felony or crime involving dishonesty while employed by the company is prohibited.
- **Gambling**: Gambling on company premises is not allowed.
- **Property Damage**: Careless or willful destruction or damage to company property or the property of other employees or clients is prohibited.
- **Falsification of Records**: Falsifying company records, including financial records, travel expense vouchers, medical bills, and other employee forms and documents, is not allowed.
- **Unauthorized Information Use**: Unauthorized dissemination of personal information or improper use of such information is prohibited.
- **Job Refusal**: Refusal or failure to carry out a reasonable job assignment or request after being warned that failure to do so could result in termination is not acceptable.
- **Excessive Personal Use**: Excessive use of company telephone, email, or Internet for personal purposes is prohibited.
- **Disruptive Behavior**: Any behavior that is seriously disruptive to the normal flow of company business and is unlawful in nature is not allowed.

Employees with concerns about any of the above misconduct should discuss them with their department head to find a solution. If issues persist and the employee is consistently found at fault, disciplinary procedures will be followed based on the severity of the situation. The Head of HR, in consultation with the department head, will make the final decision. For information security and data privacy violations, the matter will be reported to the CIO/CISO and HR Head, following the Security Incident Response procedure.

### 5. Disciplinary Actions

The following disciplinary actions may be taken based on the severity of the misconduct:

- **Oral Warning**: This will describe the behavior that necessitated the warning and express continued support for the employee, with the expectation of improvement.
  
- **Written Warning**: This will include reasons for the manager’s dissatisfaction and supporting evidence. The employee will have the opportunity to defend their actions at the time the warning is issued.
  
- **Termination**: Depending on the severity of the situation and consistent breaches of discipline, the employee may be terminated from the company.

### 6. Exception(s)

There are no exceptions to this policy.
