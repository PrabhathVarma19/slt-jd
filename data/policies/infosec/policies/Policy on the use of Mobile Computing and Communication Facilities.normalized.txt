## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the mobile computing and communication facilities policy is to:
- Prevent misuse of electronic communications and computing resources.
- Ensure employees are not exposed to unethical behavior such as error, fraud, defamation, breach of copyright, unlawful discrimination, illegal activity, privacy violations, and computing resource service interruptions.

## 1.1 Mobile Computing
Mobile Computing facilities include, but are not limited to:
- Laptops, mobile phones, and other portable computing devices.
- Media, CD-ROMs, DVDs, disks, pen drives, and memory storage devices.
- Video conferencing.

## 1.2 Communication Facilities
Communication facilities include, but are not limited to:
- E-mail.
- Internet.
- Mobile phones, VOIP phones, Skype, Microsoft Teams, Kaizala.
- Forums, blogs, and other internet-based communications tools.

## 2. Objectives
The objectives of this Policy are to:
- Facilitate the efficient, effective, responsible, and lawful use of Trianz computing and communication facilities.
- Safeguard the interests of Trianz and all authorized users of its computing and communication facilities.
- Provide guidelines and instructions to authorized users in the appropriate use of Trianz’s computing and communication facilities.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Trianz Associates and contractors having access to Trianz Mobile computing and communication facilities.

## 4. Policy Statements
- Trianz shall ensure effective measures are in place to protect confidential information regarding the use of mobile computing and communication facilities.
- Protection measures are established to avoid unauthorized access to or disclosure of confidential data stored and processed by these facilities.
- Mobile devices, including laptops, smartphones, and tablet computers, can enhance productivity but also present significant risks to Trianz’s information assets.
- Employees may use their own equipment (BYOD) to connect to Trianz's network and systems, provided they install specific security controls as per the BYOD Policy.
- IT will review devices and remove any Trianz data if the relationship with Trianz terminates, devices change, or in similar situations.
- It is recommended to use encryption and other protection strategies (e.g., device management software, access controls, remote wiping) on any mobile device containing Confidential or Highly Confidential Information. Mobile devices accessing Trianz email must be protected using Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Physically secure any mobile devices used to access or store Trianz information. Laptops must never be left unattended.
- Associates must not take pictures with mobile phones/cameras inside any ODCs provided to customers as part of the MSA/SOW.
- It is recommended not to connect mobile devices containing Trianz information to any unsecured network without an up-to-date firewall or other security controls in place.
- Unsecured networks include home networks, hotel networks, open or for-pay wireless hotspots, convention networks, or any other network not approved or controlled by Trianz. Refer to the BYOD policy for more details on associate-owned devices.
- Remote geolocation capabilities must be maintained for all mobile devices.
- A remote wipe feature must be enabled on all Trianz-managed mobile devices to securely erase data if the device is lost.

### 5. Roles & Responsibilities

#### 5.1 All Associates
- Associates are responsible for all activities on mobile computing and communication facilities initiated by their ID.
- If a user suspects that the security of their computing and communication facilities has been breached or compromised, they must report it to the IS Team.

#### 5.2 IS Team
- The IS Team is responsible for responding to and investigating reported breaches.

#### 5.3 InfoSec Team
- The InfoSec Team conducts a bi-annual review of the policy on the use of mobile computing and communication facilities.
