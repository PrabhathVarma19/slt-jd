## 1. Purpose
This document specifies the requirements that must be met by suppliers in handling, management, storage, and processing of information belonging to Trianz and Trianz clients.

## 2. Scope
This policy is applicable to all third-party contract suppliers, including cloud service providers and vendors offering products and services to Trianz and its clients.

## 3. Policy Statements
- Trianz Information security requirements for mitigating risks associated with supplier access to the organization’s assets are agreed upon and documented with the supplier.
- Trianz must identify and mandate information security controls to be addressed by suppliers, including:
  - Identification and documentation of the types of suppliers, including cloud service providers.
  - Ranking of suppliers based on the confidential and personal data being exchanged and the financial value.
  - Vendor risk assessments performed by respective functional coordinators for critical vendors.
  - Annual information security and privacy risk assessments of all critical vendors conducted by respective functional coordinators.
  - Review and feedback on the risk assessment form by the information security team to the respective functional coordinator.
  - Coordination with the vendor to address review feedback by the respective functional coordinator.
  - Definition of allowed access types for suppliers.
  - Minimum information access (least privilege) granted to suppliers.
  - Obligations of suppliers to protect organizational information.
  - Handling of incidents associated with suppliers.
  - Business continuity for information provided by either party.
  - Documentation of security requirements and controls in agreements signed by both parties.

### 3.1 Information Security Policy for Supplier Relationships
- Information security requirements must secure processes and procedures to ensure appropriate controls implemented within the organization or by suppliers, cloud service providers, and cloud service customers.
- Access control for sensitive information must be accurately defined, managed, and monitored.
- Awareness training for both the organization’s staff and supplier staff handling or interacting with sensitive data must be provided.
- Service transitions must be documented, including procedures for secure data transfers and availability as supplier relationships evolve.

### 3.2 Addressing Security within Supplier Agreements
- All relevant information security requirements must be established and agreed upon with each supplier that may access, process, store, communicate, or provide IT infrastructure components for Trianz data.
- Supplier agreements must be documented to clarify both parties’ obligations regarding security requirements, including:
  - Trianz data classification requirements applicable to the supplier.
  - Definition of acceptable uses for the data handled by the supplier.
  - Processes and procedures for monitoring compliance with contract requirements.
  - A "right to audit" the supplier or regular access to external assessments, as applicable.
  - Conflict and defect resolution procedures.
  - Required screening, training, or other obligations for the supplier’s staff.
  - Use of sub-contractors to provide services and extension of security requirements to them.
- Early identification of risks in the procurement phase is essential to clearly define roles, responsibilities, and expectations in agreements or contracts.
- Suppliers/vendors must ensure compliance with security patches and verification of Vendor Risk Assessment/Periodic vendor audits.

### 3.2 Cloud Services Security Measures
The following information security measures must be implemented by the cloud service provider:
- **Malware Protection**
- **Backup**
- **Cryptographic Controls**
- **Vulnerability Management**
- **Incident Management**
- **Security Testing and Auditing**
- **Authentication and Access Control**

It is essential to ensure that there is no disagreement between the cloud service provider and the customer regarding these measures.

### 3.3 Information and Communication Technology Supply Chain
- Agreements with suppliers must include requirements to address information security risks associated with information and communications technology services and product supply chains.
- Trianz's information and communication technology supply chain pertains to both software and hardware technology usage.
- A process must be established to identify products or services that are critical and require increased scrutiny.
- Trianz must address risks related to components or services becoming unavailable or no longer supported.
- A risk assessment of information processing and storage vendors/suppliers must be performed at the time of their termination or when services are no longer required.

### 4. Exceptions
- There are no exceptions to this policy.

### 5. ISO Control Mapping(s)
The following controls are mapped to ISO 27001:2022:

- **5.19**: Processes and procedures must be defined and implemented to manage information security risks associated with the use of suppliers' products or services. (Document: Supplier Security Policy)
  
- **5.20**: Relevant information security requirements must be established and agreed upon with each supplier based on the type of supplier relationship. (Document: Supplier Security Policy)
  
- **5.21**: Processes and procedures must be defined and implemented to manage information security risks in the information and communication technology (ICT) supply chain. (Document: Supplier Security Policy)
  
- **5.22**: The organization must regularly monitor, review, evaluate, and manage changes in supplier information security practices and service delivery. (Document: Supplier Security Policy)
