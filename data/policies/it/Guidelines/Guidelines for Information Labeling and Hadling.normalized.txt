# Guidelines for Information Labeling and Handling

## 1. Information Classification

- **Public**: Accessible to the public at large.
- **Internal**: 
  - Read access will be granted to all Trianz employees for internal use only.
  - Employees must not share information with any third party or outsourced employees unless authorized by their departmental head.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Access must be limited to named authorized individuals only.
  - Access by external parties must be subject to a non-disclosure agreement and a business need to know.
  - Any authorized person taking a photocopy of confidential information must do so personally or have the document photocopied in their presence.
  - When printing documents, the individual must collect the printout immediately after executing the print command.

## 2. Access Restrictions

- Employees shall not share information with anyone outside their own department.
- Handling of information must be based on the classification of the Information Asset, including circulation, maintenance, and discarding of data.
- Data owners must be identified.
- Access should be granted based on the confidentiality of the data type.

## 3. Storage of Information

- **Public**: 
  - No specific security control requirements.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Documents must be stored on desktops with password protection.
  - E-mails containing confidential information must be downloaded to the local personal folder immediately upon receipt to prevent storage on mail servers.
  - Hard copies of confidential documents should preferably be kept under lock and key.
  - For backup purposes, all confidential documents should be stored in the respective user’s folder on the file server.

### 1. Password Protection
- Hard disks should preferably be password protected.

### 2. Confidential Document Storage
- Confidential documents should not be stored in public directories of the file server.
- The complete hard drive of a desktop shall not be shared on the network. 
- On a need basis, selected folders on the hard drive can be shared, and access should be given to named individuals.
- Data shall be encrypted; additional controls may be required depending on the sensitivity of the data, although other access controls might suffice.

### 3. Storage of Information
- **Public Control Area**: No specific security control requirements.
- **Internal Use Only**: Information should preferably be stored in a personal storage shelf, which should be locked.
- **Confidential**: 
  - Information should be kept in a separate cabinet, preferably a fireproof cabinet. 
  - The authority to manage the cabinet will be assigned to a person nominated by the department head.
  - Access to the information/cabinet shall be granted by the department head via email.
  - Any confidential information that is to be kept or retrieved from the cabinet must be done in front of the designated person.
  - Any hard copy documents that cannot be reproduced (e.g., legal contracts, third-party contracts) should also be kept preferably in fireproof cabinets.

### 4. Labeling of Information (Documents and Records – Soft Copy)
- **Public**: No labeling required.
- **Internal Use Only**: The document/record (in electronic form) must contain the information classification logo with the classification identified as ‘Internal Use Only’ at the start of the document/record.
- **Confidential**: 
  - The document/record (in electronic form) must contain the information classification logo with the classification identified as ‘Confidential’ at the start of the document/record.
  - The document/record (hard copy) should have the information classification logo as a cover page or as part of the title page with the classification identified as ‘Confidential’.
  - The information owner is responsible for labeling the document. If there is any doubt while classifying the information, the owner should seek concurrence.

### 1. Email Communication of Confidential Information
- All email communications containing confidential information must have 'Confidential' as the first word in the subject line.
- The attached document must be classified as “Internal Use Only” and this classification should be marked on the first page.

### 2. Labeling of Information Medium
- No labeling is required for information mediums such as diskettes, CDs, or hard copies.
- The information medium must be marked 'Confidential' if applicable.

### 3. Addressing/Packaging
- No security control requirements are necessary for addressing/packaging.
- The storage medium must have two envelopes/layers of packaging:
  - The outer envelope/layer must:
    - Show the recipient's name and address.
    - Be marked 'To be opened by addressee only'.
    - Show the name and phone number of the sender.
  - The inner envelope/layer should be marked as “Confidential”.

### 4. Dispatch and Distribution of Information (except Email)
- No security control requirements are necessary for dispatch and distribution.
- Packaging must ensure physical protection of the item.
- Information can be dispatched or distributed to anyone outside the department, third party, or outsourced party by:
  - Normal mail service or by any courier.
  - Hand delivery or approved courier.
- Printed information sent through internal mail, external mail, or by courier must be sent by trusted courier or registered mail, with a method that provides tracking.
- Circulation of Internal Use Only documents to any third party or anyone outside Trianz requires approval from the head of the concerned department.
- The document must detail the data retention period based on regulatory, sensitivity, and information classification.
- Distribution is to named individual(s) only.
- Confidential information cannot be taken out of Trianz premises in the form of media like hard copies, CDs, floppies, USBs, etc., without prior permission from the department head.

### 4.1 Data Classification
- The validity of data classification must be checked periodically. If there are any changes, data should be reclassified accordingly.

### 4.2 Dispatch and Distribution of Information (Electronic Mail)
- **Internal Emails**: Sending emails containing information to internal employees does not require explicit approval from Trianz authority.
- **External Emails**: Circulation of internal documents to any third party or anyone outside Trianz requires approval from the head of the concerned department via email, with a copy marked to them.
- **BCC Restrictions**: BCC while emailing is not permitted for "Confidential" documents.
- **Confidential Marking**: When sending documents to authorized persons, they must be marked as "Confidential" in the subject or body of the email, ensuring visibility.
- **Data Encryption**: Data must be encrypted during transfer using appropriate encryption methodologies.

### 4.3 Voice Communication
- **Security Controls**: No security control is required for voice communication.
- **Discussion Restrictions**: Conversations must not be held on speakerphones or during teleconferences unless all participants confirm that no unauthorized persons are nearby who might overhear.

### 4.4 Disposal of Information
- **Draft Documents**: These documents may not be used for draft (reverse side) printing.
- **Destruction of Documents**: Documents may be destroyed manually, ensuring they cannot be easily reconstructed.
- **Retention Period**: Information should only be destroyed or deleted after the minimum retention period, when it is obsolete or no longer needed.
- **Hard Copy Confidential Documents**: Any hard copy confidential document to be destroyed must be torn into small pieces, preferably using a shredder at the time of destruction.

### 1. Data Disposal Policy

- Any confidential information present on a CD that cannot be deleted from electronic media must be physically destroyed.
- Any hard disk containing confidential information must undergo low-level formatting to ensure that the information cannot be recovered.
- The secure disposal of data, including disks, must be documented.
