## 1. Introduction
E-mail is extensively used for all communication in TRIANZ. The following guidelines aim to reduce e-mail risks and suggest strategies for secure e-mail usage and position both employees and the organization in a professional manner before internal and external audiences.

## 2. Objectives
This guideline covers the following aspects:
- Authorization of Usage
- E-mail Administration
- Directions for usage
- E-mail System Integrity
- Disclaimer

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all individual users (Employees, Consultants, and others associated with TRIANZ) who use the E-mail services provided by TRIANZ.

## 4. Guidelines

### 4.1 Authorization of E Mail Usage
- Only employees and other persons authorized by the appropriate TRIANZ authority may use TRIANZ’s E-mail systems and resources.
- HR will initiate User Access Registration on the Local E-mail server for new employees by raising a request to the IS Service Desk.
- The IS team will create a unique user profile within 2 working days from the request date, ensuring the username is unique (Firstname.lastname).
- HR will initiate the deregistration of user access to the Local E-mail Server upon termination, resignation, or transfer of an employee by raising a request to the IT Service Desk.
- The IS team will deactivate email access on the same day, subject to approval from the Delivery Head or Project Manager.
- For departing employees, HR will raise a request for deactivation, which will be handled by the IS Team.

#### 4.1.1 User Identification and User Password Management
- All authorized users will receive a unique user identification (user-id).
- Access to the mailing system will be based on the user id and a password following the standard policy (firstname.lastname).
- Password management must comply with the Password Policy of TRIANZ.

#### 4.1.2 Setting Usage Norms & Protection
- The IT team will administer the Local e-mail system with appropriate settings, including mailbox size and maximum size of outgoing attachments.

### 4.1.2 Email Attachment Size Limits
- Users are allocated the following email storage limits:
  - General users: 50 GB to 100 GB
  - Functional Heads / Project Managers: 100 GB
  - Delivery Heads / Directors and above designated management staff: 100 GB
- All email attachments must not exceed 10 MB in size.
- Exceptions to the attachment size limit must be explicitly authorized by the IS Manager/CIO.
- All mail servers must implement appropriate virus protection, spam filters, spoof detection, and ransomware protections, which should be updated periodically to ensure total protection.

### 4.1.3 Company Property
- All messages generated or handled by TRIANZ's electronic communication systems, including backup copies, are considered the property of TRIANZ and not the users.
- For client-administered email systems, property rights may belong to either TRIANZ or the client, depending on the terms of engagement.

### 4.2 Directions for Usage

#### 4.2.1 Purpose of Use
- The use of TRIANZ’s email system must be related to business needs.
- Incidental and occasional personal use of email is allowed if it does not incur direct or indirect costs for TRIANZ.
- Any personal use of TRIANZ’s email resources is subject to the provisions of this guideline.

#### 4.2.2 Importance of Email
- TRIANZ considers email an important and effective means of communication.
- The content and speed of email responses are crucial for conveying a professional image and providing good client service.

### 4.3 Email System Integrity

#### 4.3.1 No Default Protection
- Employees are advised not to send sensitive information in plain text, even though TRIANZ's electronic communication systems are encrypted for internal emails.

#### 4.3.2 Monitoring of Communications
- TRIANZ respects employees' rights and their reasonable expectation of privacy.
- Routine monitoring of email is not conducted; however, monitoring may occur as permitted by law for maintaining system integrity and effective operation.
- TRIANZ reserves the right to access and disclose the contents of users’ emails without user consent, as permitted by law.
- TRIANZ may intercept and filter incoming and outgoing emails/attachments, especially executables/documents, in anticipation of virus threats or to check for data leaks.
- Users who employ encryption devices to restrict access to their emails must provide access to such communications upon request, except for client-administered email systems.

#### 4.3.3 Inspections and Disclosure of Communication
- TRIANZ reserves the right to inspect and disclose email contents during investigations of misconduct or misuse, or to respond to legal processes, to locate substantive information necessary for business operations.

#### 4.3.4 No Guaranteed Message Privacy
- TRIANZ cannot guarantee the privacy of email communications.
- Employees should be aware that emails, while protected against known vulnerabilities, can still be forwarded, intercepted, printed, and stored by others.

#### 4.3.5 Statistical Data
- TRIANZ collects statistical data about email communications consistent with generally accepted business practices.

### 4.3 Monitoring of E-mail Communications
- The IS Server team is responsible for monitoring E-mail communications to ensure continuous availability and reliability of these systems.
- Note: This monitoring does not apply to client-administered E-mail systems.

### 4.4 E-mail Forwarding
- Email forwarding to personal email IDs is strictly prohibited. Users are not allowed to use auto email forwarding to personal IDs for corporate emails.
- Users should exercise caution when forwarding messages, as some information is intended for specific individuals and may not be appropriate for general distribution.

### 4.5 E-mail Etiquette

#### Do’s:
- Write well-structured emails with a short and descriptive subject heading.
- Use an informal style; keep sentences short and to the point.
- Start emails with “Hi,” “Hello,” or “Dear,” followed by the recipient's name.
- End messages with “Warm Regards,” “Best Regards,” or “Thanks and regards.”
- Use a spell checker before sending emails.
- Include your name, job title, company name, and contact details in your signature, if appropriate.
- Compress attachments whenever possible and avoid sending unnecessary attachments.
- Clearly state the expected action when forwarding emails.
- Mark emails as important/priority only if they truly are.
- Delete any unnecessary email messages.
- Periodically purge messages no longer needed for business purposes from personal email storage.

#### Don’ts:
- Do not write emails in all capital letters.
- Do not subscribe to newsletters or news groups without prior permission from your supervisor.
- Do not use email resources for personal monetary gain or for commercial purposes not directly related to TRIANZ’s business.
- Do not use offensive or abusive language in emails.
- Do not send copies of documents/software in violation of copyright laws.
- Do not capture, intercept, or open emails not meant for you, except as required for authorized employees to diagnose and correct delivery problems.
- Do not use email to harass or intimidate others or interfere with their ability to conduct TRIANZ’s business.
- Do not use email systems for any purpose restricted or prohibited by law or regulations.
- Do not forward emails to public email service providers (e.g., Hotmail, Yahoo).
- Do not engage in “spoofing,” which involves constructing an email to appear as if it is from someone else.
- Do not engage in “snooping,” which involves obtaining access to the files or emails of others.
- Do not attempt unauthorized access to email or breach security measures on any email system.
- Do not send mass emails addressed to all employees without consulting the IS Group.
- Do not make or post indecent remarks, proposals, or materials.
- Do not reveal or publicize confidential or proprietary information, including financial information, new business and product ideas, marketing strategies, client lists, and access codes.
- Do not use email systems for charitable endeavors, private business activities, or entertainment purposes unless expressly approved by TRIANZ.
- Never create the appearance or reality of inappropriate use of the email system.
- Do not send mass greeting cards.
- Do not forward emails to multiple addresses unless it serves genuine business purposes.

### 4.6 Security of Email
- Each employee of TRIANZ is assigned a unique email ID for official use only.

### 4.1 Email Usage Agreement

- Employees must sign an agreement regarding the usage of email at the time of joining and are required to strictly adhere to this agreement.

### 4.2 Responsibilities

- The IS Team is responsible for administering email services and email backups.
- Only designated IS team members have ownership of the Global admin delegation.

### 4.3 Account Management

- User IDs and email accounts will be deleted at the time of employee separation.

### 4.4 Email Size Limitations

- The size of outgoing emails is limited to 10MB. Emails exceeding this size will be automatically deleted.

### 4.5 Spam Policy

- Spam is considered an unacceptable use of the STPI IP network. Spam includes the following activities:
  - Posting a single message or similar messages to more than five online forums or newsgroups.
  - Collecting responses from unsolicited emails.
  - Sending emails with charity requests, petitions for signatures, or any chain mail-related materials.
  - Sending emails that do not accurately identify the sender, the sender’s return address, and the email address used for login.
  
- Users can report any suspected spam emails using the Outlook report button.

### 4.6 Virus Protection

- Email communication with the outside world is protected. Email attachments are automatically scanned on the mail server by antivirus software.
