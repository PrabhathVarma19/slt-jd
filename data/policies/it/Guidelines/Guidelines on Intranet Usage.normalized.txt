## 1. Objective
The company utilizes the Intranet as a critical resource for business processes. Trianz has developed systems and procedures to ensure that security is maintained for the Intranet at all times, with a uniform code of conduct.

## 2. Scope
These guidelines apply to all users of Trianz and cover:
- Local Area Network (LAN) and Wide Area Network (WAN) connected with Trianz’s workstations and servers, including applications and system software.
- All systems under the jurisdiction and/or ownership of Trianz, including hired systems.
- All workstations and servers supplied by Trianz customers, as long as they are operated from within Trianz premises.

## 3. Reference(s)
- Information Security Code of Conduct

## 4. Rules on Intranet Usage
- All services on the Intranet of Trianz LAN and WAN network are for the purpose of information update and dissemination among employees and selected third-party vendors. 
- All information posted to the Intranet must have a designated owner, and all postings or modifications must be approved.

### Default Access
- Default Intranet access is granted to all employees based on their department, with corresponding default username and password communicated to the user.
- Employees will have private protected VLAN/Isolated Wi-Fi connection based on their project requirements. All Intranet access will be secured with Zscaler webproxy.

### Access to Other Resources of Intranet
- For access beyond the default, users must raise a Service Request to the IS Department with approval from their respective Head of Department. Access will be granted as per the approval for the specified duration.

### Authentication & Authorization
- All Intranet access is governed by proper authorization, and access to all Intranet servers will be protected and managed through PAM.

### Information Dissemination
- Intranet information is for dissemination only to authorized individuals. Users must not forward this information to others without seeking approval through the appropriate channel.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
- Any user, as defined in the “Scope” section, is responsible for using Trianz’s Intranet resources in compliance with this policy.
- The IS Manager is responsible for reviewing these guidelines annually.
- The designated Information Security Officer/Designated member of the Security Forum, along with the Human Resources group, is responsible for distributing these guidelines.
