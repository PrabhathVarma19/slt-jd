## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to define the acceptable use of equipment and computing services.

## 2. Objective
The objective of this policy is to describe the acceptable use of information technology equipment and services at Trianz.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Trianz managed business systems, Trianz provided Client Services, and Trianz developed products.

## 4. Policy Statement
- Activities that are illegal under local, state, federal, or international law while utilizing TRIANZ owned resources are considered unacceptable with no exceptions.
- This policy applies to all stakeholders working for Trianz products and services.

## 5. Acceptable Use
- Associates may install necessary utilities like software or applications only after receiving approval from IS and InfoSec & Data Privacy Assurance.
- All associates must respect and protect the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of Trianz information and resources.
- All associates/users must respect and protect the intellectual property rights of others by following copyright laws.
- Users must take ownership and report all security-related incidents using the designated tool.
- Users must use Trianz assets for official purposes only.
- Associates may use their mobile phones to access Outlook, VPN tokens, etc., in a secure manner and must adhere to the MDM policy.
- Associates may use personal laptops for official use by adhering to the BYOD policy.
- Associates may browse safe internet sites by adhering to the internet user policy.

## 6. Unacceptable Use

### 6.1 System and Network Activities
- Violating the rights of any person or company protected by copyright, trade secret, patent, or similar laws, including the installation or distribution of pirated software not appropriately licensed for use by TRIANZ, is prohibited.
- Unauthorized copying of copyrighted material, including digitization and distribution of photographs, music, or software for which TRIANZ or the end user does not have an active license, is strictly prohibited.
- Exporting software, technical information, encryption software, or technology in violation of international or regional export control laws is illegal. Consult appropriate management prior to exporting any questionable material.
- Introducing malicious programs into the network or server (e.g., viruses, worms, Trojan Horses, email bombs) is prohibited.
- Revealing or sharing account/access credentials with others or allowing others to use your account is prohibited.
- Using a TRIANZ computing asset to procure offensive content or transmit material that violates POSH or hostile workplace laws in the user's local jurisdiction is prohibited.
- Making fraudulent offers of products, items, or services originating from any TRIANZ account is prohibited.
- Making statements about warranty or commitment, expressly or implicitly, without proper authority is prohibited.

### 5. Network Security Rules
- Actions that cause disruptions to network communication, unauthorized access, network sniffing, pinged floods, packet spoofing, denial of service, and forged routing information for malicious purposes are prohibited.
- Port scanning or security scanning is prohibited unless prior notification is made to the IT department.
- Executing network monitoring that intercepts data not intended for the employee's host is prohibited unless it is part of the employee's normal job duties.
- Circumventing user authentication or security of any host, network, or account is prohibited.
- Interfering with or denying service to any user other than the employee's host is prohibited.
- Using any program, script, command, or sending messages to interfere with or disable a user's terminal session is prohibited.
- Providing information about TRIANZ employees to parties outside TRIANZ is prohibited.
- Use of cloud computing services for work purposes must be formally authorized by the IT Manager/CIO, who will ensure that security, privacy, and IT management requirements are addressed by the vendor.
- Agreements for cloud services requiring user consent must be reviewed and approved by the CISO/CIO.
- Use of cloud services must comply with cloud security and data privacy controls for handling personally identifiable information, corporate financial data, or any other data owned or collected by TRIANZ.
- Personal cloud service accounts may not be used for storing, manipulating, or exchanging company-related communications or company-owned data.

### 6.2 Email and Communications Activities
- Sending unsolicited email messages, including junk mail or advertising material to individuals who did not request such material, is prohibited.
- Any form of harassment via email, telephone, or paging is prohibited.
- Unauthorized use or forging of email header information is prohibited.
- Soliciting email for any other email address, other than the poster's account, with the intent to harass or collect replies is prohibited.
- Creating or forwarding chain letters, Ponzi, or pyramid schemes is prohibited.
- Use of unsolicited email from TRIANZ's networks to advertise any service hosted by TRIANZ or connected via TRIANZâ€™s network is prohibited.
- Posting the same or similar non-business-related messages to large numbers of Usenet newsgroups (newsgroup spam) is prohibited.

### 7. Exceptions
- No exceptions are permitted.
