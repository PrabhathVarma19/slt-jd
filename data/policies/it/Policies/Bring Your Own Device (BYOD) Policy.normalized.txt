## 1. Purpose
- Define the acceptable usage policy for employee-owned personal devices (e.g., Desktops, Laptops, Mobile phones, Smartphones, Tablets).
- Protect the security and privacy of Trianz’s and its clients’ information and infrastructure assets from BYOD devices.

## 2. Objective
- Allow employees to use their personally owned devices for work-related activities, including accessing emails, connecting to the corporate network, and accessing corporate apps and data, with appropriate approval and adequate security control.

## 3. Scope
- All employee-owned personal devices must be registered with the IS department and must have supervisory approval for use within the Project.
- The policy applies to all stakeholders working for Trianz products and services.

## 4. Policy Statements
- Employees must agree to the terms and conditions set forth in this policy and install required Mobile Application Management (MAM) software onto their mobile phones to connect to the company network and/or access policy-permitted information/infrastructure assets.
- Employees must sign a “BYOD No-Objection and Exclusivity Agreement” and ensure validation from the IS team to use Laptops, Desktops, Tablets, etc., as BYOD.
- No other personal devices, other than those specified, are permitted inside Trianz premises or allowed to access Trianz's network and computing resources on premises, in the cloud, from home, from client locations, or during transit.

### 4.1 Acceptable Usage of Laptops/Desktops/Tablet
- Employee’s system must be validated by the IS team prior to connecting to Trianz’s network.
- BYOD is not allowed for any access to Client environments through Trianz VPN.
- BYOD can only be allowed with approval from the CIO/CISO and explicit approval from the Client if the resource has access to the client’s network.
- BYOD must be configured with all necessary security controls as per BYOD SOP and Conditional Access (refer to IS SOP).
- BYOD users must sign a “No-Objection & Exclusivity Agreement” for Trianz information and deliverables on their BYOD device.

### 4.2 Acceptable Usage of Mobile Phone/Smart Phone
- Employees may use Personal Mobile Phones/Smartphones to access the following company-owned resources:
  - MAM managed Microsoft applications such as email, calendars, documents, etc.
  - Mobile service provider messages for receiving One Time Password (OTP).
  - Corporate authorized soft tokens/OTP generators.
- No other information/infrastructure assets of Trianz or its clients should be accessed using personal Mobile Phones/Smartphones, except for publicly accessible resources and cloud-based solutions available from anywhere.

### 4.2 Mobile Phone Use Policy

- Associates must comply with all government and applicable regulations regarding the use of mobile phones/smartphones in their respective countries.
- Acceptable business use of mobile phones is defined as activities that directly or indirectly support the business needs of Trianz.
- Mobile phone devices must not be used to:
  - Store or transmit illicit materials.
  - Engage in outside business activities.
  - Store or transmit proprietary information belonging to Trianz or another company.
- Users are prohibited from:
  - Sending unsolicited email messages, including "junk mail" or advertising material to individuals who did not request it.
  - Any form of harassment via email, telephone, or paging, regardless of message language, frequency, or size.
  - Unauthorized use or forging of email header information.
  - Soliciting email for any address other than the poster's account with the intent to harass or collect replies.
  - Creating or forwarding "chain letters," "Ponzi," or other "pyramid" schemes.
  - Using unsolicited email originating from within Trianz's networks.
  - Posting the same or similar non-business-related messages to large numbers of UseNet newsgroups (newsgroup spam).

### 4.3 Security

- Personal and company-provided mobile phones must be password protected according to the company’s password policy to prevent unauthorized access.
- Associates are responsible for ensuring their personal or company-provided mobile phones are updated with manufacturer or network-provided patches.
- Associates must not load pirated software or illegal content onto their personal devices used for official purposes.
- Associates must not store company data or client data on personal or company-provided mobile phones.
- Access to company data is limited based on user profiles defined by IT and is automatically enforced.
- Corporate data on an employee’s BYOD device may be remotely wiped if:
  - The device is lost.
  - The employee terminates their employment.
  - IT detects a data or policy breach, a virus, or a similar threat to the security of the company’s data and technology infrastructure.

### 4.4 Risks/Liabilities/Disclaimers

- The company reserves the right to disconnect personnel or company-provided mobile phone devices or disable services without notification.
- Lost or stolen personal or company-provided mobile phones must be reported to the company immediately. Employees are responsible for notifying their mobile carrier upon loss of a personal mobile device.
- If an employee suspects unauthorized access to company data via a mobile device, they must report the incident immediately.
- Employees are expected to use their personal or company-provided mobile phones ethically and adhere to the company’s acceptable use policy.
- Employees are personally liable for all costs associated with their mobile devices.
- Employees assume full liability for risks, including partial or complete loss of company and personal data due to operating system crashes, errors, bugs, viruses, malware, or other software or hardware failures.
- Trianz reserves the right to take appropriate disciplinary action, up to and including termination, for noncompliance with this policy.

### 5. Roles & Responsibilities

- **All Associates**
  - Obtain client approval to use BYOD.
  - Obtain CIO/CISO’s approval.
  
- **IS Operations Team**
  - Validate the system prior to connecting BYOD to Trianz’s network.
  
- **InfoSec & Compliance Team**
  - Review the Security and Privacy policies.
