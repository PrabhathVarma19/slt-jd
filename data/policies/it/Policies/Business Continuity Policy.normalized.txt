## 1. Purpose
This policy aims to establish an organization-wide Business Continuity Management Systems (BCMS) Program aligned with ISO 22301:2012, ISO 20000-1:2018, and ISO 27001:2022 standards to minimize risks to Trianz operations, resources, reputation, and shareholder value.

## 2. Objective
1. Proactively define processes and procedures to ensure essential ICT functions continue during and after significant events and effectively respond to emergencies that may affect the company.
2. Prepare for and respond to unforeseen events, minimizing the impact on mission-critical services and protecting the health and safety of Trianz employees and subcontractors at Trianz facilities.
3. Establish an Emergency Response and Crisis Management structure across all locations to facilitate local resumption of activities and deployment of supporting or recovery resources.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all people (Trianz employees, contractors, and vendors), Trianz processes, and Information Technology (IT) resources owned, managed, or operated by Trianz across all geographies.

## 4. Policy Statements
- Trianz shall establish a BCMS framework for the company, including its subsidiary operations and operating units, to guide its business continuity efforts.
- Trianz shall prioritize the safety of all employees and subcontractors working at Trianz facilities.
- Each Trianz location shall implement and document a Business Continuity Plan under the guidance of top management, driven by the location's crisis management team.
- The BC plan will establish a crisis management structure across all Trianz locations to facilitate local resumption of activities and deployment of supporting or recovery resources.
- Trianz shall identify legal, regulatory, and contractual commitments related to Business Continuity requirements and ensure compliance with each requirement.
- All Trianz facilities shall undergo a risk assessment to evaluate the impact of potential risks/disruption scenarios on business functions, resources, and infrastructure, ensuring appropriate procedures are in place to withstand the impact of risks/disruptions.
- Trianz shall perform a detailed Business Impact Analysis (BIA) for all critical business functions, resources, products, infrastructure, and ICT functions that, if disrupted, would materially impact business operations and the company's reputation.
- Trianz shall establish granular ICT continuity objectives and requirements derived from the comprehensive BIA. These objectives will ensure the recovery of critical IT systems within defined Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) through effective redundancy solutions, robust backup procedures, and a designated Disaster Recovery (DR) site.

### 4. Business Continuity Management

1. Trianz shall test the Business Continuity Plan(s) annually, including suppliers of critical goods or services, and make modifications based on test results.
2. Trianz shall establish Disaster Recovery (DR) Planning as a key component of the Business Continuity Management System (BCMS), focusing on restoring critical information systems.
3. All associates and stakeholders must be informed of the Business Continuity Plans applicable to their sites, departments, and roles following the invocation of the BC Plan.
4. The Business Continuity Policy, along with the associated Framework and BCMS Program elements, shall be reviewed semi-annually or in response to significant changes in the company’s operating environment.
5. Trianz account-level Business Continuity Plan (BCP) coverage is limited to key strategic accounts, while location-specific BCPs shall cover all Trianz locations.
6. Trianz shall conduct BCP tests, including:
   - Call tree tests bi-annually.
   - Tabletop simulations at least once a year, depending on client requirements, for critical strategic projects.
   - Products and network-level ISP tests for location-specific BCPs.

### 5. Roles & Responsibilities

1. Trianz Business Continuity assurance is responsible for organization-wide guidance, policy, and oversight of the BCM program.
2. Trianz Operating Units and locations are responsible for developing, implementing, maintaining, and testing their BCM Program.
3. Each Trianz location must document the Business Continuity Plan using the approved standardized template provided by Business Continuity assurance.
4. Each Trianz location shall have a Business Continuity Plan addressing life and safety issues, including evacuation and shelter-in-place procedures, and must document business activities conducted at each location. The Business Continuity assurance team is responsible for completing these plans.
5. Client-related BCP/DR requirements shall be governed by respective client contracts.
6. For account-level, product-level, and project-specific BCPs, the respective delivery manager/project manager is responsible for creating, managing, and maintaining the BCP program, with assistance from delivery assurance. The InfoSec BCP Single Point of Contact (SPOC) shall provide support in creating the BCP.
7. The Business Continuity assurance team is responsible for ensuring this policy document is reviewed at least annually.
