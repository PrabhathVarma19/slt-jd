## 1. Introduction
The ISDP Assurance Team enables Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance across Trianz and ensures compliance with various ISO Standards and Regulations.

### 1.1 Objectives
- Ensure that the organization’s Information Security & Privacy Management System is established, implemented, maintained, and improved.
- Demonstrate the organization’s commitment to securing organizational and client information assets against vulnerabilities and threats.

## 3. Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance Roles, Responsibilities and Authorities

### 3.1 Chief Information Officer (CIO)
- Responsible for overall Information/Cyber Security and Privacy for Trianz.
- Sets clear vision, mission, and direction for the Information Security and Data Privacy Assurance Team.
- Communicates the cybersecurity posture and any risks to stakeholders both internally and externally on a periodic basis.

### 3.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
- Ensures that ISMS policies and procedures are defined and implemented throughout the organization.
- Ensures that information security policies and recommended practices are updated in a timely manner to reflect all current modifications.
- Reviews the information security policy at least annually.
- Ensures that information security awareness is provided to all employees.

### 3.2 Responsibilities for Information Security

- Ensure that security requirements for new information processing facilities are identified and approved.
- Define an appropriate technical architecture for the security of IT infrastructure and monitor compliance.
- Arrange necessary resources and skills for conducting periodic information security reviews.
- Assess and ensure disaster recovery and business continuity preparedness for IT systems.
- Define appropriate security controls for all applications in consultation with the application owner.
- Maintain a copy of risk assessment documentation and a list of identified vulnerabilities as noted by the Information Security Assurance Council Specialists.
- Maintain and review all critical incidents and their corresponding resolution timeframes.
- Conduct ongoing Risk Assessments of Trianz Information Assets.
- Perform Vulnerability Assessment Tests of Trianz network and Cloud infrastructure on a quarterly basis.
- Involve in-house or external security specialists as needed for specific information security requirements.
- Decide on the applicability of the disciplinary policy in cases of reported security incidents.
- Provide directives to ensure organization-wide information security.
- Delegate executive responsibilities for ensuring information security and privacy.
- Take necessary measures to maintain the security of information systems.
- Ensure staff is adequately trained to meet the organization's security and privacy requirements.
- Define responsibilities and establish procedures to promptly detect, investigate, report, and resolve information security incidents.
- Ensure provisions are in place for the continued protection of information systems resources.
- Approve vendors for selecting appropriate information security products.
- Conduct Root Cause Analysis (RCA) for information security incidents and exceptions.
- Approve all Information Security Management System (ISMS) and Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) related policies and process documents.

### 3.3 Information Security Group Responsibilities

- Ensure that ISMS functions within their respective business groups comply with Trianz Information Security Policy and procedures, as well as data privacy policies and procedures.
- Maintain data classification according to Trianz Information classification labeling and handling.
- Monitor adherence to Trianz Information Security and Data Privacy Policies and Procedures.
- Report any information security incidents and data privacy breaches to the Information Security Assurance Council and respective supervisory authorities, e.g., report security breaches to CERT-in within 6 hours.
- Coordinate with other members of the Information Security Assurance Council for continuous implementation of ISMS and PIMS.
- Be accountable on behalf of their respective groups for ISMS and PIMS.
- Provide suggestions to the Information Security Assurance Council for enhancing information security and data privacy measures.
- Ensure that information security procedures relevant to business groups align with Trianz Information Security Policy and Data Privacy Policy.
- Ensure mapping of contractual obligations to the Master Service Agreement (MSA) and provide walkthroughs to project teams.
- Ensure third-party risk and compliance with vendors.

### 3.4 Data Privacy Assurance Group

- The roles and procedures for the Data Privacy Assurance Group will be defined in subsequent sections.

### 1. Responsibilities

#### 1.1 Breach Notification
- When Trianz acts as a Processor, the Data Privacy Representative (DPR) will notify the Controller of Data Privacy Breaches within 48 hours of the breach and will follow further actions as directed by the Controller (Client).
  
- When Trianz acts as a Controller:
  - The DPR/CISO will notify the Supervisory Authority of Data Privacy Breaches within 72 hours of the incident.
  - The DPR/CISO will notify CERT-In of Data Privacy Breaches within 6 hours of identifying the breach, depending on the criticality of the incident (Refer to Security Incident Response Procedure).
  - The DPR/CISO will notify data subjects as advised by the Supervisory Authority and as required by data privacy regulations.

#### 1.2 Consent Procedure
- Ensure that the consent procedure is followed for processing personal and sensitive data.

#### 1.3 Cross Border Data Transfer Procedure
- Assist the project/delivery/support team in ensuring compliance with Cross Border data regulations.
- Review Data Portability requests from all functions and maintain a central register.
- Address Data Portability requests within 30 days.
- If data cannot be shared within 30 days, update the data subject within 30 days and document the communication.
- Ensure that data is shared within 90 days of the request from the data subject.

### 2. Data Protection Impact Assessment Procedure
- Ensure the availability of Data Privacy Diagnostic or threshold assessment where applicable at project initiation.
- Support the Project Manager and Delivery Manager in conducting a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) or Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) where applicable.
- Assist in identifying risks, prioritizing risks, and developing risk mitigation plans, including tracking their effectiveness and timelines.
- Ensure that mitigation plans are implemented and their effectiveness is documented.

### 3. Data Subject Rights Procedure
- Conduct an annual review of Data Subjects Rights procedures for adequacy.
- Perform a quarterly review of the Data Subject Rights log for its adequacy.

### 4.1 Compliance to Regulations and Corrective Actions
- Ensure compliance with regulations and implement corrective actions as needed.

### 4.2 Withdrawal of Consent Procedure
- Modify or withdraw consent forms and manage the status of processing personal data.

### 4.3 Privacy by Design Procedure
- Support the implementation of Privacy by Design and Default by identifying data protection requirements.
- Incorporate applicable geography-related data protection requirements.

### 4.4 Privacy Notice Procedure
- Conduct a yearly review of privacy concerns of data subjects and implement corrective actions.
- Review the Privacy Notice and sample privacy consent notice for adequacy on a yearly basis.

### 4.5 Retention of Records Procedure
- Review the Retention of Records Procedure on a yearly basis.

### 4.6 Audits & Compliance
- Facilitate internal and external audits and ensure compliance with privacy/security assessments, certifications, or seals.
- Support privacy/security assessments by clients.
- Ensure project compliance with various security standards and regulations, such as ISO 27701 and SOC.
- Ensure organizational compliance with applicable privacy laws, including GDPR and CCPA.
- Document and present internal review and audit findings to leadership.

### 4.7 Policies and Procedures
- Maintain compliance with the Data Privacy Management System (DPMS) and Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) for privacy policies, procedures, and templates.

### 4.8 Risk and Privacy Impact Assessment
- Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) to identify privacy risks and manage them for IT services projects and corporate functions.
- Support third-party risk management for privacy/security by assisting in contract management processes and privacy risk assessments of vendors.
- Assess internal applications for privacy/security risks and facilitate privacy risk management.

### 4.9 Privacy Trainings
- Roll out required amendments to Data Privacy Trainings within the organization.

### 4.10 Continual Upgradation & Interactions with Security and Privacy Professionals
- Continually learn, share knowledge, and foster skill exchange.
- Proactively identify opportunities to improve reporting quality and usability of information.
- Implement applicable privacy laws for amendments or new business geographies related to privacy laws.
- Interact with internal and external security and privacy professionals as needed.

### 4.11 Maintenance of Personal Data Inventory
- Maintain a Personal Data Inventory for corporate functions in the role of Data Controller.

### 4.12 Audits Responsibilities
- Ensure yearly planning for internal and external audits.
- Conduct bi-annual internal audits and yearly external audits for:
  - Quality Management System
  - Information Security Management System
  - Privacy Information Management System
  - Service Management System across Trianz locations in India, US-Virginia, and US-California.

### 3.6 Business Continuity

- Establish a Business Resilience and Crisis Management structure across all location levels to facilitate local resumption of activities and deployment of supporting or recovery resources.
- Design and establish a Business Continuity Management (BCM) framework for the company, including its subsidiary operations and operating units, to guide their business continuity efforts.
- Conduct Business Impact Analysis and BCP risk assessments for any Trianz offices and critical functions, and develop continuity plans.
- Regularly test the business continuity plans for key engagements and IT systems, and report results to management.
- Periodically review and update the business continuity plans in response to any changes.
- Develop and provide staff training on BCM through awareness sessions, eLearning, and mailers.
- Collaborate with delivery and corporate functions to develop and implement best practices for protecting and restoring data and systems in the event of natural disasters and security/privacy incidents.
