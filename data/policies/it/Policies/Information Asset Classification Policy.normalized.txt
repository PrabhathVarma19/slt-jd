# INFORMATION ASSET CLASSIFICATION POLICY

## Statement of Confidentiality
The information contained in this document is internal to Trianz. It shall not be disclosed, duplicated, or used for any purpose other than that stated herein, in whole or in part, without the express written consent of Trianz.

## Information Classification
- **Public**: Information will be made available to the public at large. Reasonable care will be taken to prevent any damage or alteration to such information (e.g., website content, marketing brochures, press releases).
- **Internal**: Information is for internal use only. It will be available to all employees of the organization and only to authorized persons outside the organization (e.g., clients or third-party service providers). Adequate security measures shall be taken to prevent unauthorized access and/or modifications.

## 1. Introduction
The information classification scheme is essential for protecting informational assets by assigning value and safeguarding confidentiality, integrity, and availability. The level of security afforded to company data depends directly on its classification level. Organizations shall consider confidentiality, availability, integrity, and privacy requirements when categorizing assets. This ensures that the classification scheme balances business needs and security requirements for each category of information.

## 1. Objective
The purpose of this policy is to establish the key principles of information classification for both electronic and physical forms. This policy supports the "need to know" principle, ensuring that information is protected from unauthorized disclosure, use, modification, and deletion.

## 2. Scope
This policy is applicable to all Information Assets of Trianz located on premises, offshore, client premises, or on cloud infrastructure.

## 3. Activities performed

### 3.1 Policy
- All associates are responsible for ensuring that company information assets are protected and stored by classifying the information properly as per the Information Classification policy.
- All assets shall be catalogued and tagged in the asset inventory.
- Respective function owners are responsible for ensuring that the Information Assets are classified according to the information classification policy.
- Information assets at Trianz are classified into four categories:
  - **Public**: Information will be made available to the public at large, with reasonable care taken to prevent damage or alteration.
  - **Internal**: Information is for internal use only, available to all employees and authorized external persons, with adequate security measures to prevent unauthorized access and modifications.

### 3. Confidential

- Confidential information will only be available to authorized persons, both within and outside the organization (e.g., clients or third-party service providers).
- Distribution of confidential information will be on a “need-to-know” basis only.
- Adequate security measures must be implemented to prevent unauthorized access.
- Examples of confidential information include:
  - Business plans
  - Marketing strategies
  - Human resources database
  - Strategic plans
  - Customer-specific data

### 4. Restricted

- A restriction is defined as a limitation.
- Any asset containing Personal or Sensitive Information protected by law will be classified as restricted.
- Assets classified as restricted must be protected with the highest level of access control and security measures, such as encryption and rights management.
- Examples of restricted information include:
  - Candidates' resumes
  - Employee profiles
  - Documents containing individual financial information
  - Individual health information (e.g., payrolls, income tax files, insurance claims, website visitor database, vendor information, biometric information)

### 4. Roles and Responsibilities

1. **Associates**: Responsible for classifying documents according to the policy.
2. **Function/Product Owner**: Ensures that information is classified according to the policy.
3. **CISO/CIO**: Approves the policy.

### 5. Measurement and Reporting

- Measure the number of documents identified versus the number of documents classified.

### 6. Compliance and Monitoring

- Ensure that 100% of information classification is completed.

### 7. Exception(s)

- There are no exceptions.

### 8. ISO Control Mapping(s)

- **Organizational Controls**:
  - **5.12 Classification of Information Control**: Information shall be classified according to the organization's information security needs based on confidentiality, integrity, availability, and relevant interested party requirements.
  - **5.13 Labelling of Information Control**: An appropriate set of procedures for information labeling shall be developed and implemented in accordance with the information classification scheme adopted by the organization.
