### 6. Planning

#### 6.1 Actions to Address Risks and Opportunities

##### 6.1.1 General
- When planning for the information security management system, Trianz shall:
  - Identify issues and determine risks and opportunities to achieve intended outcomes related to confidentiality (C), integrity (I), availability (A), and privacy (P) of information assets.
  - Ensure transparency in operations and meet customer information security needs.
  - Develop and maintain Business Continuity Planning (BCP) strategies.
  - Reduce undesired effects such as breaches of information security requirements, data loss, or integrity issues.
  - Plan for continual improvement by reviewing feedback from stakeholders, customer feedback, internal audit results, and the accomplishment of objectives.
  - Implement risk mitigations to counter negative effects, determined through risk assessment methodologies.

- Refer to the Risk & Opportunity Management Procedure and the Business Continuity and Disaster Procedure for further details.

##### 6.1.2 Information Security and Privacy Risk Assessment
- During risk assessment, the risk value of an asset is calculated as the product of Business Impact and Probability of Occurrence.
- The values for Confidentiality (C), Integrity (I), Availability (A), and Privacy (P) are rated on a scale of 1, 3, and 9.
- Asset identification is conducted for all assets containing information value in each department.
- A unified risk assessment methodology is developed to conduct risk assessments.
- Risk owners are identified during the risk assessment process.
- All threats applicable to an asset are documented, and vulnerabilities for each information asset are identified along with associated threats.
- The risk assessment methodology will be applied to all identified assets.

- Refer to the Risk and Opportunity Management Procedure in the ISMS Procedure folder for more information.

##### 6.1.3 Information Security Risk Treatment
- Based on the Risk Assessment, actions will be taken by Delivery and Corporate Functions/Support to address identified risks.

### 6.1.4 Information Security and Privacy Objectives and Planning to Achieve Them

- Top Management shall establish and direct the ISDPA team to define information security objectives at relevant functions and levels, in alignment with the information security policy.
- These objectives must be measurable to ensure that information security requirements are met, taking into account results from risk assessments and risk treatment history.
- Information Security Objectives shall be documented, detailing:
  - The plan to achieve the objectives
  - Responsibilities
  - Resources required
  - Evaluation mechanisms
- The ISDPA team is responsible for communicating with all relevant stakeholders and for reviewing and monitoring the Information Security Objectives.

### 7.5 Documented Information

#### 7.5.1 General
- There are two types of documents at a high level:
  - **Blue Book Documents**: Includes Policies, Manuals, Group Manuals, Procedures, Guidelines, Checklists, and Templates.
  - **Project and Corporate Function Documents**:
    - Documents prepared during project execution, such as PM Workbooks, SRS, Design, Test Plan, Test Cases, etc.
    - Documents related to Corporate Functions, including InfoSec and Compliance, HR, Recruitment, Learning & Development, Admin, Purchase, and IS Operations.

#### 7.5.2 Creating and Updating
- At Trianz, records are identified in each procedure and maintained by process owners for ISMS purposes.
- These records serve as evidence for implementing the ISMS and for assessing effectiveness over time.
- The internal audit team verifies these records against procedures to check adequacy.
- Most records are maintained in either soft copy or hard copy format, with a backup process in place for copies.

### 7.5.3 Control of Documented Information

- All Bluebook documents must be controlled through proper reviews and approvals.
- Changes to controlled documents require review and approval by the appropriate authority before release for use.
- All evolving documents are version controlled, ensuring only relevant approved versions are available for use.
- All documents are accessible online for reference by authorized personnel in a common database.
- Controlled documents are identified by a unique Release Identification (Release ID).
- Documents of external origin must be suitably identified and made available only for intended purposes and users.
- Any Blue Book process document used for proposals, customer offsite usage, or distribution outside the organization is considered uncontrolled once it leaves Trianz premises, and change control does not apply.
- Blue Book Process Artifacts that are no longer in use must be moved to an obsolete folder.
- All records identified by projects and corporate functions must be generated and stored as specific records.
- Records related to projects and corporate functions must be retained for 7 years from the date of creation. Records older than 7 years will be disposed of at the discretion of the Project Manager or Corporate Function Manager.
- All ISMS documents and records will be protected and controlled according to the Documented Information Procedure, available in the Common Support Process Folder in Blue Book.

1. The status of actions from previous management reviews.
2. Changes in external and internal issues relevant to the ISMS.
3. Changes in needs and expectations of interested parties relevant to the ISMS.
4. Feedback on information security performance, including trends in:
   - Nonconformities and corrective actions.
   - Monitoring and measurement results.
   - Audit results.
   - Fulfillment of information security objectives.
5. Feedback from interested parties.
6. Results of risk assessment and status of the risk treatment plan.
7. Opportunities for continual improvement.

- The outputs of the management review shall include decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to the ISMS.
- The organization shall retain documented information as evidence of the results of the management review.

- ISMS effectiveness is reviewed periodically, typically with risk assessment reviews conducted every six months and management reviews conducted annually. These reviews will include inputs from security audits, interested parties, customer feedback, incidents, and trends related to ISMS objectives.

- The effectiveness of controls is measured from various sources, including:
  - Information security incidents.
  - Breaches.
  - Objectives tracking.

#### At Trianz, reviews are conducted to assess:
- The level of residual risk and acceptable risk, considering changes to:
  - Organizational changes, such as changes in processes and IT infrastructure.
  - Technology adopted for service delivery.
  - Business objectives and processes required for service delivery.
  - Identified threats during ISMS operation over time to check the effectiveness of implementation.
  - External events, such as changes in the legal or regulatory environment and changes in social climate, as well as contractual obligations related to information security.

- Any need for conducting a reassessment of risks is evaluated during the management review.

**10. Improvement**

**10.1 Nonconformity and Corrective Action**
- The Trianz ISAC team will take action to eliminate the causes of nonconformities related to the implementation and operation of the Information Security Management System (ISMS) to prevent recurrence.
- Documented procedures for corrective action will include the following requirements:
  - Identify nonconformities in the implementation and/or operation of the ISMS.
  - Determine the causes of nonconformities and assess potential impacts and damage to the ISMS.
  - Evaluate the need for actions to ensure nonconformities do not recur.
  - Determine and implement necessary corrective actions.
  - Record the results of actions taken.
  - Review the corrective actions taken.
  - Identify potential nonconformities and eliminate them to prevent occurrence.

**10.2 Continual Improvement**
- The organization has defined and documented an Information Security policy and objectives. The ISMS Measurement Framework is designed with these policies and objectives in mind.
- A Top Management Review will be conducted to assess the implementation and effectiveness of the ISMS, leading to required corrective or preventive actions for identified improvements.
- Internal audits, customer audits, and certification body audits will be conducted periodically to ensure adherence to the Blue Book and facilitate continual improvement.
- Corrective and preventive actions will be taken to prevent the recurrence and occurrence of nonconformities.
- Lessons learned from security experiences of other organizations, including clients, vendors, suppliers, partners, and advisories, will be applied.
- Employees are encouraged to initiate process improvements by submitting a ‘process change request’ to the Trianz Process Improvement Group (TPIG).
- The Infosec team will evaluate process change requests and implement them in the Blue Book if deemed feasible.

**Corrective Action**
- The organization will take action to eliminate the causes of nonconformities to prevent recurrence.
- After identifying nonconformities, a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) will be conducted to identify root causes and corrective actions. The identified corrective actions will be implemented and their effectiveness verified.
- If a corrective action requires a process change, the appropriate Blue Book process artifact will be modified.

**Preventive Action**
- The organization will take action to eliminate the causes of potential non-conformities to prevent their occurrence.
- The priority for preventive actions will be determined by the ISAC based on inputs from security reviews and relevant departments.
- The effectiveness of implemented preventive actions will be verified.
