## 1. Purpose
Improper handling of removable media can lead to data loss and leakage. This policy outlines the media handling requirements to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and privacy of sensitive information.

## 2. Objective
To ensure the secure management of media to protect sensitive or personal information from intentional or accidental exposure or misuse.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Management, Employees, Contractors, and Third-Party Employees who use Trianz media, as well as all products, services, and organizational IT assets of Trianz.

## 4. Privacy
The media handling policy document is considered "confidential" and will be made available to authorized personnel with proper access control. Changes and versions of the document will be controlled.

## 5. Policy Statements

### 5.1 Media Handling
- All Trianz associates are responsible for ensuring the physical security of media.
- The use of USB flash drives is blocked for all associates; approval from the CIO/CISO is required for any exceptions.
- Personal CDs, DVDs, data tapes, hard disk drives, flash drives, external memory cards, and other devices are not allowed.
- The use of CD/DVD writers is restricted to authorized personnel; printer facilities are for official use only.
- All media must be handled with care, kept away from magnetic materials, and protected from extreme heat or pollution.
- Compliance with this policy is the responsibility of overall delivery and corporate functions.

### 5.2 Media Shipping for Work-from-home Associates
- All devices containing information must be shipped securely, following special procedures when associates are working from home.
- Shipping must be conducted from Trianz office or to/from non-office locations.
- Special controls should be implemented for media in transit to protect sensitive information from unauthorized disclosure or modification.
- A record must be maintained for incoming and outgoing physical media containing Personally Identifiable Information (PII), Personal Data, or Personal Sensitive Data.
- Packaging for media in transit must be adequate to protect contents from physical damage, in accordance with manufacturersâ€™ specifications.

### 5.3 Disposal of Media and Equipment
- Media must be disposed of securely and safely when no longer required to prevent sensitive information leakage.

### 5.3 Disposal of Media
- If reusable media is no longer required, its previous content must be deleted by low-level formatting after obtaining prior approval from the CISO/CIO.
- Disposal of sensitive items must be logged to maintain an audit trail and comply with regulatory requirements.
- All equipment containing storage media must be checked for confidential information, which should be backed up or removed prior to disposal.
- Storage devices with sensitive information must be either physically destroyed or securely overwritten, using low-level formatting instead of the standard "Delete" function.
- All licensed software installed on equipment must be removed before disposal.
- Damaged storage devices containing sensitive data must be checked for data access, after which a decision will be made to repair, discard, or destroy the device.

### 5.4 Removal of Equipment
- Equipment (including desktops, laptops, servers, VOIP phones, etc.), information, or software cannot be taken off-site without prior authorization from IS Operations and the CISO/CIO.
- A Verification Report must be prepared by an authorized IT representative and sent, either physically signed or via email, to admin for preparing a gate pass.

### 5.5 Reuse of Equipment
- Equipment shall be reused where feasible. Data and software must be securely removed, and the equipment re-provisioned for use.
- Parts may be dismantled and reused if there is potential for recovering valuable components.
- Equipment will be recycled at the unit or component level using an external disposal contract coordinated by IT Services.

### 6. Roles & Responsibilities
- **All Associates, Including Contract Employees**: Any exceptions to this policy must be requested with proper business justification and approved by the reporting manager and CISO/CIO.
- **Admin Team**: Responsible for ensuring all logistics align with this policy and related procedures.
- **IS Operations Team**: Ensures all IS operations comply with this policy and related procedures.
- **Infosec Team (CISO/CIO)**: Approves exceptions based on business justifications and the risk of information loss.
