## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish a standard for the creation of strong passwords, protection of those passwords, and the frequency of change.

## 2. Objective
Ensure a standard for the creation of strong passwords, the protection of those passwords, and the frequency of change of the passwords.

## 3. Scope
This policy covers all employees, including contract employees, who are responsible for one or more accounts (e.g., Email, Server accounts, System accounts, etc.) or have access to any resource that requires a password. The policy applies to all stakeholders of internal Trianz products and services.

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Password Construction Requirements
- Passwords must be a minimum of eight characters in length.
- Passwords must consist of at least three of the following four-character sets:
  - Lowercase alpha characters (e.g., a, b, c, d, e)
  - Uppercase alpha characters (e.g., A, B, C, D, E)
  - Numbers (e.g., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5)
  - Special symbols or punctuation characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, %, &, *, _, +, ~, ., >)
- Passwords must not be the same as the User ID.
- Passwords must expire within a maximum of 42 days.
- The 'Minimum password age' must be set to 1 or more days.
- Passwords must not be identical to the previous eight passwords.
- Passwords must not be transmitted in clear or plain text outside Trianz.
- Passwords must not be visible while typing.
- Passwords must not contain series of the same letters.
- Password resets must only be done upon the authorized user’s request.

### 4.2 Password Creation
- All user-level and system-level passwords must comply with the Password Construction Requirements in section 4.1.
- Users must not include personal information (e.g., name, phone number, social security number, date of birth, or addresses) in passwords.
- User accounts with system-level privileges must have a unique password distinct from all other accounts held by that user.

### 4.3 Password Change
- All system-level passwords (e.g., root, NT admin, application administration accounts) must be dual controlled and changed at least once every 42 days.
- All user-level passwords (e.g., Email, web, desktop computer) must be changed at least once every 42 days.
- All password change procedures must include:
  - Authentication of the user prior to changing the password (acceptable forms of authentication include answering a series of questions).
  - The new password must comply with the password strength requirements associated with the data classification for the service in question.

### 4.4 Password Protection
- Users are responsible for the security of their passwords and are accountable for any loss or compromise.
- Passwords must not be shared with anyone and must be treated as sensitive, confidential Trianz information.
- Any user suspecting that their password may have been compromised must change it immediately.

### 4.4 Password Management

- If a password is compromised, it must be changed immediately, and the incident should be reported to is@trianz.com, with a copy sent to itservicedesk@trianz.com.
- Passwords must not be included in email messages or other forms of electronic communication.
- Passwords should not be disclosed to anyone, including administrative assistants, secretaries, managers, co-workers (even while on vacation), or family members, over the phone or on questionnaires.
- Users must not write down passwords or store them in any form (paper, computer systems, or mobile devices) without encryption.
- Users should not utilize the "Remember Password" feature in applications, such as web browsers.
- A password will be locked after 5 unsuccessful login attempts.
- Default passwords for vendor or service accounts must be removed from computer systems before deployment, and new passwords must be implemented immediately upon installation.

### 4.5 Roles and Responsibilities

- **All Associates**: Responsible for changing passwords as per policy and ensuring password security. (Internal)
- **IS Operations Team**: Responsible for deploying the Password Policy and resetting passwords based on user requests. (Internal)
- **Product/Project Manager**: Publishes and maintains policy guidelines for the creation, safeguarding, and control of passwords. (Internal)
- **InfoSec & Compliance Team**: Audits adherence to the Password Policy during internal audits. (Internal)
