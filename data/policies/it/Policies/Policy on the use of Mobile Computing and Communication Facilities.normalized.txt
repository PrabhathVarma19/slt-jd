## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the mobile computing and communication facilities policy is to:
- Prevent misuse of electronic communications and computing resources.
- Ensure employees are not exposed to unethical behavior such as:
  - Error
  - Fraud
  - Defamation
  - Breach of copyright
  - Unlawful discrimination
  - Illegal activity
  - Privacy violations
  - Computing resource service interruptions.

### 1.1 Mobile Computing
Mobile computing facilities include, but are not limited to:
- Laptops
- Mobile phones
- Other portable computing devices
- Media (CD-ROMs, DVDs, disks, pen drives, memory storage devices)
- Video conferencing

### 1.2 Communication Facilities
Communication facilities include, but are not limited to:
- E-mail
- Internet
- Mobile phones
- VOIP phones
- Skype
- Microsoft Teams
- Kaizala
- Forums, blogs, and other internet-based communications tools

## 2. Objectives
The objectives of this Policy are to:
- Facilitate the efficient, effective, responsible, and lawful use of Trianz computing and communication facilities.
- Safeguard the interests of Trianz and all authorized users of its computing and communication facilities.
- Provide guidelines and instructions to authorized users for the appropriate use of Trianz’s computing and communication facilities.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Trianz Associates and contractors having access to Trianz mobile computing and communication facilities.

## 4. Policy Statements
- Trianz shall ensure effective measures are in place to protect confidential information regarding the use of mobile computing and communication facilities.
- Protection measures are established to avoid unauthorized access to or disclosure of confidential data stored and processed by these facilities.
- Mobile devices, including laptops, smartphones, and tablet computers, can provide substantial productivity benefits but also present significant risks to Trianz’s information assets.
- Trianz may permit employees and others to use their own equipment (BYOD - refer to BYOD Policy) to connect to its network and systems, provided that specific security controls are installed on the devices (as per BYOD Policy).
- IT shall review devices and remove any Trianz data if the relationship with Trianz terminates, devices or services change, or in similar situations.
- It is recommended to use encryption and other protection strategies (e.g., device management software, access controls, remote wiping in case of loss or theft) on any mobile device that contains Confidential or Highly Confidential Information. Mobile devices that provide access to Trianz email must be protected using appropriate security measures.

### 4. Security Measures for Mobile Devices

- **Authentication**: Use a password and/or other approved authentication methods to access or store Trianz information.
  
- **Device Security**: 
  - Physically secure any mobile devices used to access or store Trianz information.
  - Laptops must never be left unattended.

- **Photography Restrictions**: Associates are prohibited from taking pictures with mobile phones or cameras inside any ODCs provided to customers as part of the MSA/SOW.

- **Network Security**: 
  - It is recommended not to connect mobile devices containing Trianz information to any unsecured network without an up-to-date firewall or other security controls.
  - Unsecured networks include:
    - Home networks
    - Hotel networks
    - Open or for-pay wireless hotspots
    - Convention networks
    - Any other network not approved or controlled by Trianz.
  - Refer to the BYOD policy for more details on associate-owned devices.

- **Geolocation**: Remote geolocation capabilities must be maintained for all mobile devices.

- **Data Wiping**: A remote wipe feature must be enabled on all Trianz-managed mobile devices to securely erase data if the device is lost.

### 5. Roles & Responsibilities

- **All Associates**:
  - Responsible for all activities on mobile computing and communication facilities initiated by their ID.
  - Must report any suspected breaches or compromises of their computing and communication facilities to the IS Team.

- **IS Team**:
  - Responsible for responding to and investigating reported breaches.

- **InfoSec Team**:
  - Conducts a bi-annual review of the policy on the use of mobile computing and communication facilities.
