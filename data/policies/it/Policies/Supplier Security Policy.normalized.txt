## 1. Purpose
This document specifies the requirements that must be met by suppliers in handling, management, storage, and processing of information belonging to Trianz and Trianz clients.

## 2. Scope
This policy is applicable to all third-party contract suppliers, including cloud service providers and vendors offering products and services to Trianz and its clients.

## 3. Policy Statements
- Trianz Information security requirements for mitigating risks associated with supplier access to the organization’s assets are agreed upon with the supplier and documented.
- Trianz must identify and mandate information security controls to be addressed by suppliers, including:
  - Identify and document the types of suppliers, including cloud service providers.
  - Rank suppliers based on the confidential and personal data being exchanged and the financial value.
  - Respective functional coordinators from projects and functions shall perform vendor risk assessments for their critical vendors.
  - Conduct information security and privacy risk assessments of all critical vendors at least annually by respective functional coordinators.
  - Once the risk assessment form is obtained from the vendor, the information security team shall review and provide feedback to the respective functional coordinator.
  - The respective functional coordinator shall coordinate with the vendor to address the review feedback.
  - Define the types of access that are allowed to suppliers.
  - Ensure minimum information access (least privilege) to the supplier.
  - Specify types of obligations for suppliers to protect organizational information.
  - Outline handling of incidents associated with suppliers.
  - Ensure business continuity for the information provided by either party.
  - Document security requirements and controls in an agreement signed by both parties.

### 3.1 Information Security Policy for Supplier Relationships
- Information security requirements should secure processes and procedures to ensure appropriate controls are implemented either within the organization or by the suppliers, cloud service providers, and cloud service customers.
- Access control, especially for sensitive information, must be accurately defined, managed, and monitored.
- Awareness training for both the organization’s staff and supplier staff that handle or interact with sensitive data shall be addressed.
- Document service transitions, including procedures for secure data transfers and availability as the supplier relationship changes over time.

### 3.2 Addressing Security within Supplier Agreements
- Establish and document all relevant information security requirements agreed upon with each supplier that may access, process, store, communicate, or provide IT infrastructure components for Trianz data.
- Supplier agreements should ensure there is no misunderstanding regarding both parties’ obligations to fulfill relevant security requirements and should include clear and concise information regarding:

### Trianz Data Classification Requirements for Suppliers

1. **Acceptable Uses of Data**: Define acceptable uses for the data handled by the supplier.

2. **Compliance Monitoring**: Establish processes and procedures for monitoring compliance with contract requirements.

3. **Right to Audit**: Maintain a right to audit the supplier or have regular access to external assessments, as applicable.

4. **Conflict and Defect Resolution**: Implement procedures for conflict and defect resolution.

5. **Staff Obligations**: Require screening, training, or other obligations for the supplier’s staff.

6. **Sub-Contractor Security**: Extend security requirements to sub-contractors providing services. Address risks early in the procurement phase to clearly define roles, responsibilities, and expectations in agreements or contracts.

7. **Security Patch Compliance**: Ensure compliance with security patches by suppliers/vendors and verify adherence to Vendor Risk Assessment and periodic vendor audits.

### Information Security Roles and Responsibilities for Cloud Service Customers

- Define information security roles and responsibilities related to cloud services, including:
  - Malware protection
  - Backup
  - Cryptographic controls
  - Vulnerability management
  - Incident management
  - Security testing and auditing
  - Authentication and access control

### Information and Communication Technology Supply Chain

1. **Supplier Agreements**: Include requirements in agreements with suppliers to address information security risks associated with information and communications technology services and product supply chains.

2. **Technology Use**: Trianz's information and communication technology supply chain pertains to the use of both software and hardware.

3. **Critical Product Identification**: Establish a process to identify products or services that are critical and require increased scrutiny.

4. **Risk of Unavailability**: Address risks associated with a component or service becoming unavailable or no longer supported.

5. **Vendor Risk Assessment**: Perform risk assessments of information processing and storage vendors/suppliers at the time of their termination or when services are no longer required.

### 4. Exceptions

- There are no exceptions to this policy.

### 5. ISO Control Mapping

- **Category of Control**: Organizational Controls
  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 5.19 Information security in supplier relationships
    - **Document Name**: Supplier Security Policy
    - **Description**: Define and implement processes and procedures to manage information security risks associated with the use of supplier's products or services.

  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 5.20 Addressing information security within supplier agreements
    - **Document Name**: Supplier Security Policy
    - **Description**: Establish and agree on relevant information security requirements with each supplier based on the type of supplier relationship.

  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 5.21 Managing information security in the ICT supply chain
    - **Document Name**: Supplier Security Policy
    - **Description**: Define and implement processes and procedures to manage information security risks associated with ICT products and services in the supply chain.

  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 5.22 Monitoring, review, and change management of supplier services
    - **Document Name**: Supplier Security Policy
    - **Description**: Regularly monitor, review, evaluate, and manage changes in supplier information security.
