## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to establish management direction and high-level objectives for change management, ensuring control over changes to the organization, business processes, information processing facilities, and systems that affect information security. The policy aims to mitigate risks related to:
- Information corruption and/or destruction
- Disruption and/or degradation of computer performance
- Productivity losses
- Reputational risk
- Ensuring that changes in cloud services do not affect the services.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all parties operating within Trianz’s network environment or utilizing Information Resources, including:
- Cloud service provisioning and consumption
- Data networks, LAN servers, and personal computers (stand-alone or network-enabled) at Trianz offices and production-related locations
- Systems under Trianz's jurisdiction and/or ownership, including cloud environments
- Authorized personal computers, laptops, mobile devices, and servers accessing Trianz’s data networks and Cloud Networks.

All employees are subject to this policy.

## 3. Policy
Changes to information resources must be managed and executed according to a formal change control process. This process ensures that proposed changes are:
- Reviewed
- Authorized
- Tested
- Implemented
- Released in a controlled manner
- Monitored for status.

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities
1. **Change Management - Cloud Service Provider**
   - Responsible for implementing and maintaining this policy.
   - Must provide:
     - Categories of changes
     - Planned date and time of changes
     - Technical description of changes to the Cloud Service and underlying systems
     - Notification of start and completion of changes
     - Ensure that changes dependent on Peer Cloud Service Providers are communicated to the cloud service customer.

2. **Change Management - Cloud Service Customer**
   - Must consider any changes made by the Cloud Service Provider.

### 5. Operational Requirements

#### 5.1 Change Documentation
- All change requests must be logged in a standardized central system, regardless of approval status.
- Documentation must include change request details, authorization, and outcomes.
- A documented audit trail must be maintained at the Business Unit Level.
- No individual may make changes to production information systems without approval from authorized personnel.
- All change items must be included in either the Master Service Agreement (MSA) or Service Level Agreement (SLA).

#### 5.2 Change Classification
- Change requests must be prioritized based on:
  - Benefits
  - Urgency
  - Effort required
  - Potential impact on operations

#### 5.3 Change Testing
- Changes must be tested in an isolated, controlled, and representative environment before implementation to:
  - Minimize effects on business processes
  - Assess impact on operations and security
  - Verify that only intended and approved changes are made

#### 5.4 Changes Affecting SLAs
- The impact of changes on existing SLAs must be assessed.
- Changes to SLAs must be controlled through a formal change process, including contractual amendments where applicable.

#### 5.5 Approval
- All changes must receive approval prior to implementation.
- Approval criteria include:
  - Change request submitted by an authorized user
  - Impact assessment completed
  - Proposed changes tested
- Major changes must be communicated to the information security and data privacy assurance team for necessary approvals.

#### 5.6 Communicating Changes
- Users significantly affected by a change must be notified.
- User representatives must sign off on changes.
- Users are required to submit comments prior to acceptance of the change.

#### 5.7 Change Implementation
- Implementation can only occur after appropriate testing and stakeholder approval.
- Major changes are treated as new system implementations and established as projects.
- Major changes will be classified based on the effort required for development and implementation.

#### 5.8 Roll-Back Procedure
- Procedures for aborting and recovering from unsuccessful changes must be documented.
- Responsibilities for recovery and continuity must be noted if the outcome of a change differs from expected results.

#### 5.9 Emergency Changes
- Specific procedures must be established for the control, authorization, and documentation of emergency changes.
- Standard parameters will be defined for classifying changes as emergency changes.

#### 5.10 Verification of Changes
- All changes must be verified during and after implementation.
- Fall back procedures must be in place for unsuccessful implementations to revert systems to their prior state.

#### 5.11 Communication of Changes
- All changes must be communicated to stakeholders, ensuring that information security requirements have been met.

### 6. Monitoring
- Reporting metrics, documentation templates, and change implementation reports are outlined in the approved Trianz Change Management Procedure.
- Change implementation reports must be generated and maintained by the IT team and made available to Information Security and Internal Audit upon request.

### 7. Compliance
- Individuals subject to this policy who fail to comply with its provisions or amendments will face appropriate disciplinary actions.

### 8. Exceptions(s)
- Exceptions to the System and Software change management policy must adhere to the Exception handling policy.

### 9. ISO Control Mapping(s)

  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control**: 8.32 Change management
  - **Document Name as per ISO 27001:2022**: System and Software Change Management Policy
  - **Description**: Changes to information processing facilities and information systems shall be subject to change management procedures.
