## 1. Purpose
The purpose of the User Endpoint Policy is to protect information against risks introduced by using user endpoint devices, mobile computing, and communication facilities. The specific aims are:
- To prevent misuse of electronic communications and computing resources.
- To ensure employees are not exposed to unethical behavior such as:
  - Error
  - Fraud
  - Defamation
  - Breach of copyright
  - Unlawful discrimination
  - Illegal activity
  - Privacy violations
  - Computing resource service interruptions

### 1.1 Mobile Computing
Mobile computing facilities include, but are not limited to:
- Laptops
- Mobile phones
- Other portable computing devices
- Media (CD-ROMs, DVDs, disks, pen drives, memory storage devices)
- Video conferencing

### 1.2 Communication Facilities
Communication facilities include, but are not limited to:
- E-mail
- Internet
- Mobile phones
- VOIP phones
- Skype
- Microsoft Teams
- Kaizala
- Forums, blogs, and other internet-based communication tools

## 2. Objectives
The objectives of this Policy are:
- To facilitate the efficient, effective, responsible, and lawful use of Trianz computing and communication facilities.
- To safeguard the interests of Trianz and all authorized users of its computing and communication facilities.
- To provide guidelines and instructions to authorized users regarding the appropriate use of Trianz’s computing and communication facilities.

## 3. Scope
This policy applies to all Trianz Associates and contractors who have access to Trianz mobile computing and communication facilities.

## 4. Policy Statements
Trianz shall ensure that effective measures are in place to protect confidential information regarding the use of mobile computing and communication facilities. The following rules apply:
- Protection measures are in place to avoid unauthorized access to or disclosure of confidential data stored and processed by mobile computing and communication facilities.
- Mobile devices, including laptops, smartphones, and tablet computers, can provide substantial productivity benefits but also present significant risks to Trianz’s information assets.
- Employees and others may use their own equipment (BYOD - refer to BYOD Policy) to connect to Trianz's network and systems, provided they install specific security controls as per the BYOD Policy.
- IT shall review devices and remove any Trianz data if the relationship with Trianz terminates, if devices or services change, or in similar situations.
- It is recommended to use encryption and other protection strategies (e.g., device management software, access controls, remote wiping in case of loss or theft) on any mobile device that contains confidential or high-risk information.

### 4. Mobile Device Security Policy

- Mobile devices that provide access to Trianz email must be protected using Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), which includes a password and/or other approved authentication methods.
- Physically secure any mobile devices used to access or store Trianz information. Laptops must never be left unattended.
- Associates are prohibited from taking pictures with mobile phones or cameras inside any ODCs provided to customers as part of the MSA/SOW.
- It is recommended that mobile devices containing Trianz information not be connected to any unsecured networks without an up-to-date firewall or other security controls in place.
- Unsecured networks include:
  - Home networks
  - Hotel networks
  - Open or for-pay wireless hotspots
  - Convention networks
  - Any other network that Trianz has not approved or does not control.
- Remote geolocation capabilities must be maintained for all mobile devices.
- A remote wipe feature must be enabled on all Trianz-managed mobile devices to securely erase data if the device is lost.
- Ensure all endpoints are periodically updated with the latest versions and definitions.
- The use of removable devices, including removable memory devices, is restricted, and physical ports (e.g., USB ports, SD cards) must be disabled.

### 5. Roles & Responsibilities

- **All Associates**:
  - Responsible for all activities on mobile computing and communication facilities initiated by their ID.
  - Must report any suspected breaches or compromises of their computing and communication facilities to the IS Team.

- **IS Team**:
  - Responsible for responding to and investigating reported breaches.

- **InfoSec Team**:
  - Conducts a bi-annual review of the policy on the use of mobile computing and communication facilities.
