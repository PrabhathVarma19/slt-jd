## 1. Introduction
The purpose of the Asset Management procedure is to establish a formal, structured, and precise process for classifying and managing assets in accordance with the Information Security Policy and Asset Management Policy.

## 2. Objectives
The Asset Management procedure aims to:
- Establish a structured framework for managing information assets effectively.
- Define roles and responsibilities for asset management.
- Ensure accountability for asset identification, classification, and protection.
- Ensure accountability for information assets across all business functions.
- Ensure adequate resources for effective implementation and continuous improvement of asset management practices.
- Maintain a comprehensive asset inventory, including hardware, infrastructure, equipment, and licenses.
- Improve visibility into the actual cost of assets for better financial planning.
- Ensure detailed asset insights to assess the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) of IT services.

## 3. Scope
This procedure applies to:
- All employees, contractors, part-time and temporary workers, and those employed by others to perform work, regardless of geographical location.
- All assets available in all forms, including but not limited to physical and electronic forms.
- All assets available on premises, including those with vendors, suppliers, partners, or on cloud.

## 4. Assets
Assets are used to deliver services within Trianz and include:
- Computer systems
- Cloud services
- Network hardware
- Software used to process data

Some assets may also be configuration items (CIs) subject to configuration management, such as service monitoring applications or servers. Non-computerized systems holding information must be asset registered with relevant file identifications and storage locations.

### Main Categories of Assets
- Information: This includes databases and systems.

### 5. Asset Risk Assessment
- A risk assessment must be conducted for all assets of Trianz. Refer to the Risk and Opportunity Management Procedure for details.

### 6. Asset Management Procedure

#### 6.1 Asset Identification
- Identify all assets related to the project, specifying the source (Client/Trianz).
- Identify all assets related to the corporate function.

#### 6.2 Asset Register/Asset Inventory
- All assets must be documented in an asset register/asset inventory.
- The inventory should include assets in the cloud-computing environment, identifying both cloud service customer data and cloud service derived data.
- The Asset Register will be maintained by respective project managers/leads.
- Data/Information owners must classify data and objects based on data type, value, sensitivity, and criticality to the organization.

#### 6.3 Asset Tracking
- All assets must be tracked using the asset management control document tool according to the following categories:
  - **Asset Categories**: Desktop, laptop, HDD, network assets, printers, cloud assets, servers, storage, TV, projectors, RSA tokens, and critical documents/data.
  - **Procurement Mode**: Rental, purchase, direct lease, etc.
  - **Asset Status**: Active, damaged, e-waste, not working, end of life, theft, etc.
  - **Asset Location**: Any location.
  - **Vendor/Agency**: The vendor or agency associated with the asset.
  - **Asset ID**: Unique identifier for the asset.
  - **Serial Number**: Manufacturer's serial number.
  - **Assigned To**: Individual or project the asset is assigned to.
  - **Warranty/Insurance**: Details of warranty or insurance coverage.
  - **Project Code**: Code associated with the project.
  - **Asset Allocation Date**: Date the asset was allocated.
- Organization assets assigned to individuals or projects/functions must be returned upon termination or transfer of the respective individual or group.

#### 6.4 Asset Tagging
- Physical assets must be tagged according to the Guidelines for Information Labeling & Handling. Other information assets will be tagged based on respective license/subscription details.

#### 6.5 Asset Review
- All assets will be reviewed quarterly, with email communication sent to each individual asset owner.
- The purpose is to maintain asset records, including changes, updates, or deletions as required.
- Modifications to asset data may be triggered by Incident Management, Problem Management, and Configuration Management.
- The asset database will include all assets with status designations such as ordered, in storage, assigned, retired, or disposed of.
- Asset owners must acknowledge and confirm the status of the assets they own.

#### 6.6 Secure Disposal
- Organization assets must be securely disposed of when no longer needed, including physical devices, hard copies, digital assets, and cloud service customer assets containing information.
- Disposal of assets may categorize them as:
  - Obsolete
  - Unserviceable
  - To be traded in
  - To be sold
  - Missing (stolen)
  - To be disposed of by other means.
- Refer to the Data Retention and Secure Disposal Policy for further details.

#### 6.7 Acceptable Usage of Assets
- All Trianz assets shall be used only for official purposes, and project managers should ensure compliance.

### 6.8 Monitoring, Auditing, Compliance and Reconciliation of Asset Records

- Personal assets are not permitted on Trianz premises and secure areas unless project needs and requirements are met with necessary approvals.
- Compliance status for licensing and information security requirements is monitored.
- Formal inventory audits of all physical assets are conducted.
- Audits of the Asset Management System and audit reconciliation are performed.
- Audits of logical assets, including installed software on workstations and IT configurations, are conducted as required by the organization.
- Compliance with this procedure is mandatory; all project managers must ensure continuous compliance monitoring within their projects.
- Internal Audit will periodically review compliance with this procedure. Any violations will result in corrective action by management.
- An earlier review may be warranted under the following circumstances:
  - Regulatory or statutory changes or developments.
  - Results or effects of critical incidents.
  - Any other relevant or compelling reason.

### 7. Roles and Responsibilities

Each role involved in this procedure has the following main responsibilities:

- **CISO**
  - Ensures the organization's asset management strategy aligns with security policies and regulatory requirements.
  - Approves the acquisition or disposal of assets.

- **Infosec Team**
  - Ensures that the "Asset Management Policy" and "Asset Management Procedure" are communicated and understood within their respective organizational units, with CISO approval.
  - Ensures that the "Information Asset Classification Policy" and "Information Asset Management Policy & Procedure" are communicated and understood within their project teams.

- **Project Manager**
  - Responsible for classifying all project assets, obtaining approvals, and implementing adequate user access controls.
  - Designs the information system and ensures classification of the information processed by the systems.
  - Maintains and updates the assets inventory.

- **Asset Owner**
  - Accountable for classifying information assets according to the organization's Information Asset Management Policy.
  - Responsible for complying with the "Information Asset Management Policy" and "Information Asset Management Procedure."

- **User**
  - Responsible for protecting the assets entrusted to them.

- **IS Senior Manager**
  - Ensures capacity planning, acquisition, deployment, maintenance, and disposal of IT assets.
  - Manages IT asset procurement, tracks financials, and optimizes cost efficiency.
  - Ensures IT assets are protected against security incidents and properly managed during changes.
  - Maintains an up-to-date asset inventory, generates reports, and ensures visibility of IT assets.

- **IS Asset Manager**
  - Responsible for tracking assets through their acquisition and distribution.

### 9. Measurement

- **New Asset Purchases**: Count the number of new purchases made of asset types.
- **Assets in Stock**: Count the number of assets updated as "in stock," "reserved," or "deployed" from the status "in-transit" or "received."
- **Assets Lost**: Count the number of change requests for 'lost/stolen' assets over the period.
- **Assets Deployed**: Count the number of requests to deploy assets over the period.
- **Assets Disposed**: Count the number of requests for asset disposal.
