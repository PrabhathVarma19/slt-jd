## 1. Introduction
The Capacity Management is responsible for ensuring that the capacity of the IT infrastructure matches the evolving demands of the business in a cost-effective and timely manner. Capacity Management involves balancing:
- Cost against Capacity: Ensuring that purchased processing capacity is cost-justifiable and efficiently utilized.
- Supply against Demand: Ensuring that the available processing power meets current and future business demands, which may involve managing or influencing demand for specific resources.

### 8.1 Objective(s)
The objective of Capacity Management is to understand business requirements, organizational operations, and IT infrastructure to ensure that all current and future capacity and performance aspects are provided cost-effectively.

### 8.2 Scope
This procedure applies to all capacity management activities handled by project teams for customers and internal stakeholders.

## 4. Process Description

### 8.1 Capacity Management Process Flow Steps
#### 4.1.1 Manage Business Capacity Requirements
- The project manager (PM) or project team identifies the resource capacity needed to meet present and future business requirements based on the business plan/SOW. This includes:
  - Human resources
  - Technical resources (Hardware/Software)
  - Information/data

### 4.1.2 Identify and Agree SLAs
- The Project Manager (PM) and project team must identify Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for all critical utilities, service components, and services.
- SLA compliance should be monitored periodically.

### 4.1.3 Prepare and Maintain Capacity Plan
- The PM/project team is responsible for preparing a high-level capacity plan that considers the business scenario and the allocated budget.
- The PM must review the capacity plan every six months to ensure its continued suitability and identify any gaps. Refer to the PM Workbook for the Capacity and Availability Tracker.

### 4.1.4 Monitor Resource Usage to Balance Supply and Demand
- The PM/project team must monitor capacity utilization.
- The PM should assess the effectiveness and efficiency of capacity management using trend analysis to predict future capacity requirements and fine-tune utilization.

### 4.1.5 Review for Corrective Actions
- The PM/project team must plan and implement corrective actions and improvements as needed to enhance capacity management effectiveness.

### 5. Exit Criteria
- All identified action items must be closed.

### 8.1 Output
- The outputs include:
  - Capacity Plan (refer to the PM Workbook for the Capacity and Availability Management Tracker).
  - Performance and analysis reports (refer to the PM Workbook for the Capacity Trend Utilization Report).

### 6. Roles and Responsibilities
