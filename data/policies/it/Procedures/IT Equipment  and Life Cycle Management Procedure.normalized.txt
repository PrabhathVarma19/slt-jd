## 1. Objectives
- IT equipment Life Cycle Management ensures Information Security from the time of purchase until the disposal of IT hardware assets. It covers the following phases:
  - Acquisition
  - Operation & Maintenance
  - Replacement
  - Disposal

## 2. Scope
The procedure is applicable to all IT equipment assets procured for operations at Trianz, India, for all Trianz managed products and services.

### 4.2 Operation and Maintenance
- Information System & Project/Product team Administrators are responsible for the proper maintenance of the IT assets allotted to them.
- The Information System & Project/Product team maintains a list of all critical IT hardware assets along with their maintenance schedules as prescribed by vendors.
- Equipment is maintained according to the supplier’s recommended service intervals and specifications.
- Only authorized maintenance personnel are allowed to carry out repairs and service equipment.
- Records are kept of all suspected or actual faults and all preventive and corrective maintenance.
- Maintenance personnel from vendors are adequately monitored during in-house maintenance operations.
- If an IT asset needs to be sent out for maintenance, documentation is prepared to ensure traceability and accountability. Adequate packing and handling instructions are provided to the transporter to ensure physical safety during transit.
- Appropriate care is taken when sending equipment off premises for maintenance.
- Compliance with insurance policy requirements relating to IT assets is mandatory.
- Timely hardware upgrades shall be performed for both hardware and software components of IT equipment.
- Hardware upgrades apply to:
  - Processors
  - Adaptors/cables
  - Interface/Host Bus Adapters
  - Storage (Disk and Tape)
  - Memory
  - Chassis (rack, power)
- Software upgrades apply to:
  - Operating Systems
  - Database
  - File management
  - Security Devices
- Hardware and software will be tracked and reviewed monthly.

### 4.3 Replacement
- IT Equipment Hardware includes:
  - Desktops
  - Laptops
  - Servers
  - Switches
  - Routers
- IT hardware may be replaced once every 5 years.
- IT Equipment Software includes:
  - Operating Systems
  - Database Systems
  - Security Applications
- Operating systems and security-related software may be replaced once every 3 years based on business requirements, technology changes, or End of Life announcements, whichever is earlier.
- Replacement of IT hardware will be performed as per the IS Operation process document “3.6 Maintenance of Hardware.”

### 4.4 Disposal of IT Hardware Assets

- IT Administrators must approve the disposal of IT assets, including the mode of disposal, and maintain records of disposal details.
- Storage devices containing sensitive information must be either physically destroyed or securely overwritten when no longer required. For example, a damaged hard drive should be destroyed using degaussing. Evidence of information deletion must be obtained from suppliers when using their services.
- When third parties store the organization’s information, the organization should include requirements for information deletion in third-party agreements to enforce these requirements during and after the termination of services.
- Low-level formatting must be performed instead of using the standard "Delete" function.

### 4.5 Deleting Obsolete Versions, Copies, and Temporary Files

- All equipment containing storage media, such as fixed hard disks, must be checked to ensure that any sensitive data and licensed software are removed or overwritten prior to disposal.
- Damaged storage devices containing sensitive data must be assessed for data access, after which a decision will be made to repair, discard, or destroy the device.

### 5. ISO Control Mapping(s)

- **Category of Control: Physical controls**
  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control 7.13**: Equipment maintenance
    - Equipment must be maintained correctly to ensure the availability, integrity, and confidentiality of information.
  
  - **ISO 27001:2022 Control 7.14**: Secure disposal or re-use of equipment
    - Items of equipment containing storage media must be verified to ensure that any sensitive data and licensed software have been removed or securely overwritten prior to disposal or re-use.
