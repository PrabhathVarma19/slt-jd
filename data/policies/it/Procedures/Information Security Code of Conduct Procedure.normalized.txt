## 1. Objective
- The purpose of this document is to ensure effective and secure access to the Trianz information assets.

## 2. Scope
- This code of conduct applies to:
  - All employees, consultants, third-party service providers, and contractors of Trianz.
  - Third parties who have access to Trianz Information Assets.
  - All Trianz products and services.

## 3. Information Security Code of Conduct
- Employees, contractors, and third-party service providers must:
  - Sign a non-disclosure agreement prior to being given access to Trianz information assets.
  - Sign a confidentiality and intellectual property agreement to acknowledge copyright and data protection laws.
- Employees and contractors must classify all information assets produced according to the Asset Classification policy of Trianz and handle them according to the Guidelines for information labeling and handling.
- No employee shall bring any media (e.g., floppies, CDs, DVDs, USB drives, tapes, portable hard disks, laptops, cameras, video cameras) to Trianz office premises.
- Users are responsible for protecting their computer/user accounts and are accountable for any actions carried out by their assigned user IDs.
- Users must keep their passwords confidential and not share them. Temporary passwords must be changed at first logon or whenever there is a potential compromise.
- Computers (desktops and laptops) must not be left logged on if unattended for an extended period. They should be locked or have a password-protected screensaver. Users must shut down computers and peripheral devices at the end of the day.
- Computers provided by the company are for official use only and must run only company-authorized applications and programs.
- Users must not download or install any programs on their computers without prior approval from the respective Director and registration with the IT helpdesk. Screensavers must not contain anything that violates copyrights, is obscene, or is objectionable or illegal.
- All users connected to the Trianz central network must ensure their computers have the latest versions of antivirus programs. Users should report to the IT helpdesk if antivirus software is not installed or not being updated.
- Data stored on individual computers should not be backed up under regular working conditions. Business-critical data must be stored on specific servers or backed up by the IT helpdesk with Director approval.
- Users may store private data on computers at their own risk, provided it does not interfere with normal office productivity. The storage of such data should be limited.

### 5. User Responsibilities and Restrictions

- Users are prohibited from installing any applications (software, screen-savers, etc.) or additional hardware (modems, printers, etc.) on their machines. Any configuration changes on Trianz computers must be requested from the IT helpdesk.

- Users must collect their printouts from the printing device immediately after printing.

- Users are not allowed to share their user ID and password with anyone. If sharing is necessary due to emergencies, the password must be changed immediately after the emergency.

- Employees should avoid working on portable computers in public places. If it is necessary to work in public, users must ensure there is no risk of overlooking sensitive information.

- In the event of an audit, if unauthorized software (not part of the approved software list) is found on a user's system, the employee will be held responsible and may face disciplinary action.

- Company email accounts are to be used primarily for business communications. Users are prohibited from leaking confidential and internal information via email and are solely responsible for unauthorized disclosures.

- Users must not use company email accounts to initiate or forward junk emails, chain emails, jokes, or any form of spam or unsolicited emails.

- Users are prohibited from transmitting any unlawful, harassing, libelous, abusive, threatening, harmful, vulgar, obscene, or otherwise objectionable material.

- Users should never open email attachments from unknown or untrusted senders, as these may spread computer viruses. It is advisable to delete unsolicited emails with suspicious attachments.

- All personal emails must be kept in a separate folder and should be deleted periodically, preferably weekly, to avoid clogging the system.

- All outgoing emails must include the Trianz email disclaimer.

- Use of Trianz information systems to access the Internet for personal purposes is not allowed and may result in disciplinary action.

- IP phones and soft phones installed on user systems should only be used for official purposes between Trianz India and across the USA.

- Users are encouraged to use the Internet to assist in their job performance. Authorized uses include client and customer services, education and research, electronic communication for professional purposes, and procurement of information from external sources.

- Internet chat groups and newsgroups are public forums where discussing or revealing confidential information, customer data, trade secrets, or any other material described in this policy is inappropriate. Users must identify themselves honestly and accurately when participating in these forums.

- Trianz is not responsible for material viewed or downloaded by users from the Internet. Users are cautioned that many pages contain offensive or inappropriate material, and they access the Internet at their own risk.

- Users must not reveal confidential or internal company information to outsiders (third parties, family members, press, or public) unless authorized.

- Users are responsible for protecting documents containing the company’s confidential information. They must restrict the distribution of classified documents to authorized persons only and prevent unauthorized access by keeping documents secure.

- Any inappropriate use of Trianz information systems, breaches of security, or suspected security weaknesses must be reported immediately.

### 3. IT Infrastructure Security Policy

- Any observed issues with IT infrastructure at all locations must be reported immediately to the Information Security Working Group (ISWG).
- The company has implemented various security technologies to ensure the safety and security of its infrastructure. Any attempts to circumvent or undermine these security measures are strictly prohibited.
- Running unauthorized programs or tools against any Trianz system or application that could compromise its security is prohibited.
- Users must comply with Trianz's security policies and processes. Non-compliance may result in punitive actions, which can include reprimand, suspension, termination, and/or legal action.
- Violations of any points outlined in this document will be subject to disciplinary action as per the Disciplinary Action Policy.

### 4. ISO Control Mapping(s)

  - **Document Name as per ISO 27001:2022**: Information Security Code of Conduct
