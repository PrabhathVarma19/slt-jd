## 1. Introduction
This process addresses the need for a systematic approach to acquiring new systems/products that support various business processes within the organization. It details the steps involved in acquiring a Commercial Off the Shelf (COTS) software product/application/system.

## 2. Objective(s)
- Reduce the system failure rate in real-time environments through systematic acquisition of systems/products.
- Reduce the probability of unexpected system failures.
- Reduce the cost of ownership.

## 3. Scope
This process applies to all systems/products acquired to support business processes at Trianz.

## 4. Definitions/Acronyms/Abbreviations
- **COTS**: Commercial Off the Shelf
- **RFP**: Request for Proposal

## 5. Reference(s)
None

## 6. Process Description

### 6.1 System Acquisition Planning
- Initiate the system acquisition process with project definition, which includes constraints such as time, budget, and project type.
- Define managerial aspects including review boards, stakeholders, and pilot groups.
- Develop functional and non-functional requirements based on the original problem description, including user requirements, use cases, test plans, quality requirements, and technical requirements.

### 6.2 Analysis
The analysis step involves the following procedures:
- Develop an estimate of the custom development effort (in-house).
- Identify vendors.
- Create a Request for Proposal (RFP).
- Conduct testing.

### 6.3 Evaluation of System
- Conduct a final evaluation of the system to select the product based on analysis of demonstration products from identified vendors.
- Seek feedback from relevant stakeholders before making a decision on product selection.
- Refer to the Tool Evaluation Process for evaluation of tools.

### 6.4 Purchase
Once the vendor is chosen, the following procedures shall be followed:
- Conduct technical and commercial negotiations.
- Create a Purchase Order/Purchase Contract.
- Identify additional customization requirements.

### 6.5 System Modification and Customization
- After purchasing the COTS product, the acquirer and vendor shall collaborate on customization and modification activities as per the plan.
- Test the modified product against the test plan to ensure conformity to product requirements.
- Follow these sub-practices during modification and customization:
  - Planning
  - Monitoring
  - Testing

### 6.6 Piloting
- Deploy the acquired system to a subset of the systems for initial testing and feedback.

### 6.6 Pilot Testing

**Definition:**
- The acquirer of the system shall:
  - Define the pilot group size.
  - Identify the pilot group members.
  - Identify pilot test resources.
  - Outline steps to be followed by the pilot group.
  - Define pilot test cases.

**Evaluation:**
- The evaluation process includes:
  - Execution of the pilot process steps.
  - Grading of the system against defined criteria.
  - Identification of further modifications and risk factors.

**Analysis:**
- The acquirer shall analyze evaluation results and summarize defects/issues as follows:
  - **Major Defect/Issue:** A critical miss that must be fixed but is difficult to resolve.
  - **Minor Defect/Issue:** A miss that can be fixed during or after deployment.
  - **Cosmetic Defect/Issue:** A miss that should be fixed but does not necessitate a change.

### 6.7 Deployment

- The deployment of the acquired system into the production environment involves the following sub-steps:
  - **Installation:** The system is installed into production.
  - **Data Reporting:** The deployment team shall provide the acquirer with data regarding:
    - Work performed.
    - Schedule.
    - Expenses.
    - Problem reports.

### 6.8 Transition to Support

- Once the deployed system is stable and management agrees, the system transitions to operation/maintenance mode. The following sub-steps shall be followed:
  - Identify the Transition Team.
  - Prepare a Transition Plan.
  - Obtain commitments from various stakeholders.
  - Transition the system/product.
  - Monitor and control the transition process.
  - Generate a Data report.
  - Perform Transition sign-off.
