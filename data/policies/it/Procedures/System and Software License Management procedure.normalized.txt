## 1. Objective
The objective of this process is to manage the deployment, use, and monitoring of vendor software in Trianz. Systems and procedures should be developed and implemented to comply with the legal and regulatory requirements of the region in which Trianz operates.

## 2. Scope
The scope includes all internal and applicable vendor applications that are owned, operated, maintained, and controlled by Trianz, both internally and externally, either on premises or in the cloud.

## 3. Reference(s)
None

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Law Compliance
- In case of a conflict between National and/or Local laws and Trianz policy, comply with the law and notify the CFO Finance/Legal.
- The CFO Finance/Legal must ensure full compliance with laws and regulations related to information and IT applications.
- Relevant laws and regulations include:
  - Prohibition of the use of illegally acquired software.
  - Prohibition of the use of illegally acquired data.

### 4.2 License Control
- Software licenses must be controlled and maintained to protect against defaults or contractual limitations and liabilities.
- Monitor software license terms and conditions, including those for limited use, at all times.
- Do not load illegally acquired software onto Trianz computer systems.
- Products licensed for specific computers or projects/departments must not be copied to another computer or site without written authorization from the vendor.

### 4.3 License Inventory
- The IT Department must maintain original copies of licenses in a safe and secure place, with access based on authorization from the IT Head.
- Make copies of licenses and store them in a safe and secure place, separate from the original licenses.

### 4.4 Evaluation Software
- Evaluation software is acquired to assess functionality and relevance for projects or the organization.
- The following steps must be followed for managing evaluation software:
  - IT Administrators must receive and pass on the media to the IT Helpdesk, who will update the inventory and license database with details such as software name, number of licenses, date of receipt, and duration of validity.
  - The IT team must inform the requestor about the receipt of the software and receive a formal request to install it.
  - The IT team must check for viruses and install the software on the specified machine(s).
  - After installation, return the media to the IT Helpdesk, who will update the inventory and license database.
  - When the evaluation period of the software is due... (text continues).

### 4.4 Software Expiry Management
- The IT Helpdesk must inform the IT Administrator and the requestor about software expiry.
- The IT team must uninstall the software unless the requestor obtains a formal extension for the evaluation period.
- The IT Helpdesk will update the inventory and license database accordingly.

### 4.5 Freeware
- The IT Administrator, with assistance from the legal department, must verify that the freeware usage terms and conditions are valid for organizational use.
- The IT Helpdesk must be notified regarding the use of freeware software.
- The IT team must check for viruses and install the freeware on the specified machine(s).
- The IT Helpdesk must maintain a record of the freeware installed on the machines.
- The IT team must uninstall the freeware once the purpose of the freeware or the timeframe is over, whichever occurs first.

### 4.6 Periodic Audit
- Licenses will be tracked periodically by the IT Administrator. Reports will be generated to aid the tracking process and sent to the IT Head. Any discrepancies detected in the tracking report will be reported to the IT Head, who will inform the concerned user(s) and initiate appropriate action.
- User compliance with the License Management Policy will be audited twice a year during internal audits. Any discrepancies detected in the audit report will be reported to the IT Head, who will inform the concerned user(s) and initiate appropriate action.
