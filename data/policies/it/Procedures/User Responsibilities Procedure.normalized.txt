## 1. Objective
The objective of this document is to ensure that Information Asset users are aware of their responsibilities for maintaining effective access controls, particularly regarding the use of passwords and the security of user equipment.

## 2. Scope
This document is applicable to all users of information assets at Trianz.

## 3. Responsibilities

### 3.1 Password Use
- Users shall be issued a unique user ID and initial password to log on to the local system.
- Users must change their password at the time of the first login according to the following rules:
  - The password must contain alphanumeric characters and be non-dictionary.
  - The password must consist of a minimum of 8 characters.
  - The password must include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and/or special characters.
- Passwords must be changed according to the Password Security Policy.
- Users shall not share their individual passwords with any other users.

### 3.2 Clear Desk and Clear Screen
- Employees must lock away all confidential and valuable documents (both paper and magnetic) in cabinets or desk drawers when the desk is unattended for an extended period (e.g., during meetings, lunch, or overnight).
- All wastepaper containing information or data must be placed in confidential waste shredding bins. This type of wastepaper must not be disposed of in normal rubbish bins or unclassified paper recycling bins.
- Personal computers, computer terminals, and printers must not be left logged on when unattended and should be protected by key locks, passwords, or other controls when not in use.
- Employees must lock their PCs when away from the desk using “Ctrl + Alt + Delete” and log off computers and laptops when unattended. A password-protected screensaver should be activated, and at the end of work, all applications must be closed, and the workstation/laptop logged off or shut down. Laptops should be secured with a cable lock.
- All PCs, laptops, and workstations must have a password-protected screensaver with automatic activation set to 10 minutes.
- Employees shall not store any video/audio/games on their computers.
- Incoming and outgoing mail points and unattended fax machines must be protected.
- Photocopiers should be locked or otherwise protected from unauthorized use outside normal working hours.
- Users should not store confidential information on personal computers; file servers should be used for storing confidential information to ensure appropriate access restrictions and regular backups.
- Sensitive or classified information printed must be cleared from printers immediately.
- Users should collect printouts from network printers immediately and check the contents for completeness. If printing is incomplete and the user does not wish to continue, they should press the ‘Cancel Job’ button to cancel the current print job.

### 4. Security and Data Privacy Role - Delivery

- Ensure compliance with Infosec Policies and Procedures.
- Ensure compliance with Data Privacy Policies and Procedures.
- Ensure compliance with Client Specified Security Controls as defined in the Master Service Agreement (MSA) and Statement of Work (SOW).
- Ensure compliance with Client Specified Data Privacy Controls as defined in the MSA and SOW.
- Ensure adherence to standards and regulatory compliance as per Client and Trianz.
- Ensure readiness for both Internal and External Audits, as well as Client Audits.
- Ensure Infosec compliance as per the following Security Controls:

#### SECURITY CONTROLS

- **Asset Management**
  - Maintain an inventory of Client Assets.
  - Manage asset risk.
  - Maintain an inventory of Trianz Assets, including derived data like log files.
  - Make asset location decisions.
  - Ensure multi-tenancy and client data isolation for cloud projects only.
  - Implement zoning.
  - Filter incoming and outgoing traffic.
  - Utilize a Web Application Firewall.

- **Access Management**
  - Implement user-based access control.
  - Manage user access.
  - Manage privileged access.
  - Control root admin access.
  - Implement inactivation procedures.
  - Use Single Sign-On (SSO).
  - Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  - Manage passwords.
  
- **Data Encryption**
  - Encrypt data at rest, including databases, backups, and volumes.
  - Encrypt data in transmission.
  - Manage encryption keys.

- **Data Retention and Disposal**
  - Define retention periods.
  - Ensure secure disposal of data.

- **Patch Management and Malware Protection**
  - Manage backup locations and decisions.
  - Determine backup frequency.
  - Implement incremental and full backups.
  
- **Log Management**
  - Control access to logs.
  - Conduct audits of logs.

- **System Hardening**
  - Harden servers, applications, and operating systems.

- **Vulnerability and Penetration Assessment**
- **Network Security**
- **Licensing**
- **Training**
- **Change Management**
- **Configuration Management**
- **Incident Management**
- **Security Controls Contract Compliance**
- **Secure ODC Maintenance** (if applicable)
- **BCP Testing & Reporting** (if applicable)
- **Secure SDLC** (if applicable)

#### Data Privacy
- Implement Privacy by Design.
- Conduct data diagnostics.
- Maintain a personal data inventory.
- Perform Data Privacy Impact Assessments, including data diagnostics, inventory, and flows.
- Manage Data Privacy Risks.
- Notify in case of Data Privacy Breaches.
- Comply with Data Protection Contract commitments.
- Manage vendors, including signing agreements like Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA), Data Processing Agreements (DPA), Supplier Agreements, and conducting vendor security assessments.
